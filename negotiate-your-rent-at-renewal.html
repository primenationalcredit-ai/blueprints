Maximum leverage - landlord has time to consider, you have time to move if needed

üëç GOOD: When You Get Renewal Notice

üìã Your Rent Negotiation Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Research current market rents
Check Zillow, Apartments.com, Craigslist, and your building's own website for comparable units. This is your most powerful leverage! If similar units rent for less, you have a strong case.
‚úì
Step 2: Document why you're a great tenant
Make a list: always pay on time, no complaints, take care of the unit, been there X years. It costs landlords $1,000-5,000 to find new tenants - you're valuable!
‚úì
Step 3: Note any apartment issues
Aging appliances, worn carpet, peeling paint, noise issues, maintenance delays - these give you leverage. Document with photos if needed.
‚úì
Step 4: Decide your target number
Know exactly what you want before negotiating. Options: keep rent same, split the proposed increase, actual reduction, or perks (free parking, waived fees, new appliance).
‚úì
Step 5: Email your landlord (use scripts below)
Email is best - everything's in writing. Be polite, professional, and confident. Present your case clearly with the evidence you've gathered.
‚úì
Step 6: Follow up if no response in 5 days
Landlords are busy. A polite follow-up shows you're serious. If they call, let it go to voicemail and respond via email (paper trail!).
‚úì
Step 7: Negotiate and compromise if needed
If they won't meet your full request, try: "Can we meet in the middle?" or ask for non-monetary perks instead. Any savings is a win!
‚úì
Step 8: Get everything in writing
Any agreement must be in your new lease. Verbal promises mean nothing. Read the new lease carefully before signing!

üìß Email: Countering a Rent Increase MOST COMMON

Subject: Lease Renewal Discussion - Unit [#]

Hi [Landlord Name],

Thank you for sending the renewal offer. I've really enjoyed living here and would like to stay.

However, I've done some research on current rental rates in the area, and I've found that comparable units are renting for $[X] - $[Y]. Given that I've been a reliable tenant for [X years], always pay rent on time, take great care of the apartment, and have never had any complaints, I'd like to propose renewing at my current rate of $[current rent], or meeting in the middle at $[proposed amount].

I understand turnover costs can be significant, and I'd prefer to stay rather than look for a new place. Would you be open to discussing this?

Thank you for your consideration.
[Your Name]
