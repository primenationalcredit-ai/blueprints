Step 1: Gather Your Current Policy Info

Get your declarations page showing current coverage levels, deductibles, and limits. You'll match these when comparing quotes.

âœ“

Step 2: Decide Your Coverage Needs

Review the coverage guide below. Know what minimums your state requires vs. what you actually need for protection.

âœ“

Step 3: Get 5-7 Quotes

Use comparison sites PLUS direct quotes. Some companies (GEICO, State Farm) don't appear on comparison sites.

âœ“

Step 4: Compare Apples to Apples

Make sure each quote has the SAME coverage limits and deductibles. Don't compare a $500 deductible to a $1,000 deductible.

âœ“

Step 5: Check Company Ratings

Verify the insurer's financial strength (A.M. Best rating) and customer satisfaction (J.D. Power). Cheap doesn't help if they don't pay claims.

âœ“

Step 6: Ask About All Discounts

Before finalizing, ask what discounts apply: bundling, safe driver, good credit, low mileage, etc. See our Auto Insurance Discounts playbook.

âœ“

Step 7: Switch (It's Easy)

Buy new policy effective the day your current policy ends. Cancel old policy. Most handle the transition automatically.

  • State minimums are often $25,000/$50,000â€”way too low for a serious accident
  • If you cause $100,000 in damage with $25,000 coverage, YOU pay the $75,000 difference
  • 100/300/100 coverage often costs only $50-100 more per year than minimums
  • Protect your assetsâ€”if you're sued, they can come after your savings and home

ğŸ’¡ When to Drop Collision/Comprehensive

If your car's value minus your deductible is less than 10x your premium, consider dropping collision/comprehensive:

  • Car worth $4,000, deductible $500, so max payout = $3,500
  • If collision coverage costs $400/year, you're paying 11% of potential payout annually
  • After 9 years without a claim, you've paid more than you could ever receive
  • Instead: Put that premium in savings for your next car

ğŸ’¡ Best Time to Shop

  • 6 weeks before renewal: Gives you time to compare without rushing
  • After life changes: Marriage, moving, new job, paying off car
  • After tickets/accidents age off: Usually 3-5 years
  • At least once per year: Even if you don't switch, you'll know if you're overpaying
