üéØ What You'll Achieve

Most people are overpaying for auto insurance because they don't shop around! Insurance companies count on loyalty - but rates vary by hundreds of dollars between carriers. By getting 5+ quotes and asking for all available discounts, you could save $300-800+ per year for the exact same coverage.

üìã Your Insurance Shopping Checklist

‚úì
Gather your current policy information
Find your declarations page showing current coverage limits, deductibles, and what you're paying. You'll need this to compare apples to apples.
‚úì
Check your credit score (it affects rates!)
In most states, insurers use credit-based insurance scores. A better credit score = lower premiums.
‚úì
Get quotes from at least 5 different insurers
Use comparison sites AND go directly to insurers. Rates can vary by $500-1000+ for identical coverage!
‚úì
Ask about ALL available discounts
Multi-policy, good driver, low mileage, safety features, professional associations - discounts add up!
‚úì
Consider raising your deductible
Going from $500 to $1000 deductible can save 15-30% on premiums. Just make sure you can afford the deductible if you need it!
‚úì
Call your current insurer with competing quotes
Before switching, give your current company a chance to match or beat the best quote you found.
‚úì
Check company ratings before switching
Use AM Best, J.D. Power, or NAIC complaint ratios to make sure the cheaper company actually pays claims!
‚úì
Set a reminder to shop again next year
Rates change! Shop your insurance every 6-12 months to make sure you're still getting the best deal.

üìû Script: Negotiate with Your Current Insurer

Call with Your Competing Quotes

"Hi, I've been a customer for [X years] and I'm shopping my auto insurance. I've received a quote from [Competitor] for $[lower amount] with the same coverage I have now. Before I switch, I wanted to see if you can match or beat that rate, or if there are any discounts I'm not currently receiving?"

What to expect: They may transfer you to a retention specialist who has authority to offer discounts. Be prepared to actually switch if they can't match - don't bluff!

Ask for a Discount Review

"Can you please review my policy for any discounts I might be missing? I'd like to check if I qualify for: multi-policy, good driver, low mileage, safety features, paperless, autopay, or any professional/alumni discounts."

üìñ Understanding Your Coverage

Liability Coverage

Pays for damage YOU cause to others - their car repairs, medical bills, etc. This is required by law.

üí° Recommended: At least 100/300/100 ($100K per person/$300K per accident bodily injury, $100K property)

Collision Coverage

Pays to repair YOUR car after an accident, regardless of fault.

üí° Consider dropping if your car is worth less than 10x your annual premium

Comprehensive Coverage

Covers theft, vandalism, weather damage, hitting animals - everything except collisions.

üí° Same rule: consider dropping for older, low-value vehicles

Uninsured Motorist

Protects you if hit by someone without insurance (or hit-and-run).

üí° Highly recommended - about 13% of drivers are uninsured!

‚ö†Ô∏è Don't Sacrifice Coverage Just to Save Money

The cheapest policy isn't always the best. Make sure you have adequate liability coverage (at least 100/300/100) to protect your assets. The goal is to find the best RATE for good coverage, not just the lowest price.
