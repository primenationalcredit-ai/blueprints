üéØ Estate Sale Strategy Tips

üì∏ Preview Photos First

Study listing photos before going. Spot valuables, plan your priorities. Don't waste time on bad sales!

üïê Arrive Early (or Late)

Day 1 = best selection but highest prices. Last day = 50-75% off but picked over. Know your strategy!

üì± Use Your Phone

Look up items on eBay "sold listings" before buying. Know what things actually sell for!

üí¨ Ask for Deals

"Would you take $X for this?" or "Can I bundle these?" Always negotiate, especially day 2-3!

üíµ Bring Cash

Many estate sales are cash-only or give cash discounts. Small bills help with negotiating!

üöó Have Transport Ready

Big furniture finds need pickup. Have truck/van access or delivery service contact ready.

üìã Your Estate Sale Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Sign up for estate sale alerts
Create accounts on EstateSales.NET and EstateSales.org. Set up email alerts for your area. Join local Facebook estate sale groups. Never miss a good sale!
‚úì
Step 2: Learn what sells
Browse eBay "sold listings" to see what vintage/antique items actually sell for. Learn to spot mid-century modern, Pyrex patterns, valuable brands. Knowledge = profit!
‚úì
Step 3: Preview photos and plan
Before each sale, study the listing photos. Identify potential treasures. Make a list of what to look for. Don't waste time on sales with nothing valuable!
‚úì
Step 4: Hit your first estate sales
Bring cash (small bills!), phone for research, and bags/boxes. Arrive with a game plan. Check prices on phone before buying. Start with smaller purchases to learn!
‚úì
Step 5: Set up selling accounts
Create accounts on eBay, Facebook Marketplace, Mercari. Take good photos of your finds. List items at prices based on "sold" comparables.
‚úì
Step 6: List and sell your finds
Photo everything well (natural light, clean background). Write detailed descriptions. Price competitively. Cross-post on multiple platforms for faster sales!
‚úì
Step 7: Track your profits
Record what you paid, what you sold for, and net profit. Learn which categories are most profitable for you. Double down on winners!
‚úì
Step 8: Scale and specialize
Reinvest profits into more inventory. Consider specializing (furniture, vintage toys, mid-century, etc.). Build reputation and consistent sales. Grow your flipping business!
