üìã Your Signing Agent Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Become a commissioned Notary Public
Apply through your state's Secretary of State. Requirements vary - usually application, fee ($20-100), and sometimes a short exam. Takes 2-6 weeks to receive your commission.
‚úì
Step 2: Get your notary bond and stamp
Purchase your notary bond ($30-100), E&O insurance ($50-150/year - required by most signing services!), and your official notary stamp/seal ($20-40). Your stamp must match your commission exactly!
‚úì
Step 3: Complete Loan Signing Agent training
Take a certification course to learn loan documents. NNA (National Notary Association) or Notary2Pro are popular options. Self-paced online, $100-300. This teaches you everything about loan packages!
‚úì
Step 4: Pass background check and get NNA certified
Most signing services require background checks. Get NNA Signing Agent certification - it's the industry standard that opens doors with title companies and signing services!
‚úì
Step 5: Register with signing service platforms
Sign up with Snapdocs (essential!), SigningOrder, NotaryRotary, and others. More platforms = more signing opportunities. Most are free to join!
‚úì
Step 6: Complete your first signings
Accept signing orders, arrive early, guide borrowers professionally through documents, notarize properly, return packages on time. Every perfect signing builds your reputation!
‚úì
Step 7: Build 5-star ratings and reputation
Quality and reliability = more signings. Top-rated agents get first pick of orders. Be responsive, professional, and error-free. Ratings are everything in this business!
‚úì
Step 8: Build direct title company relationships
Direct work with title companies pays $150-200+ (vs $75-125 through services). Market yourself to local title companies. This is how top agents earn $10,000+/month!
