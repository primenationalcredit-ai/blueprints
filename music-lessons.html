üìã Your Music Lessons Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Assess your teaching readiness
Can you play at intermediate+ level? Can you explain concepts clearly? Patience with beginners? You don't need a degree - skill and teaching ability matter!
‚úì
Step 2: Decide in-person vs online vs both
In-person: travel to students or they come to you. Online: global reach, no travel. Most teachers do both for maximum students!
‚úì
Step 3: Set up your teaching space
For online: good webcam, quality mic, proper lighting. Test Zoom's Original Sound setting. For in-person: quiet room with your instrument.
‚úì
Step 4: Create lesson plans and curriculum
Structure for beginners through advanced. What songs/techniques at each level? Having a system makes you look professional and gets results!
‚úì
Step 5: Set your pricing and policies
Research local rates. Set cancellation policy (24-48 hour notice). Payment terms (monthly packages recommended). Lesson lengths offered.
‚úì
Step 6: Create profiles and market yourself
Sign up on platforms: Lessonface, TakeLessons, Wyzant. Post on Nextdoor, Facebook groups. Create intro video showing your playing!
‚úì
Step 7: Offer trial lessons
Discounted or free 15-30 minute trial. Meet the student, assess level, show your teaching style. Trials convert to long-term students!
‚úì
Step 8: Build your student roster
Get reviews/testimonials. Ask for referrals (offer discount for referrals). Host recitals for students. Build to 15-25 weekly students for full income!
