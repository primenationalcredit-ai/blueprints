üéØ What You'll Achieve

What's an IRA? IRA stands for "Individual Retirement Account." It's a special account YOU open yourself (separate from your job's 401k) that helps you save for retirement with tax benefits!

Why open an IRA? Tax benefits! Either you pay less taxes NOW (Traditional IRA) or pay NO taxes when you retire (Roth IRA). Either way, you keep more of your money!

The big question: Roth or Traditional? This playbook will help you decide which one is right for YOUR situation, then show you exactly how to open one in about 10 minutes!

‚öñÔ∏è Roth vs Traditional: The Simple Breakdown

üü¢ Roth IRA

  • Tax NOW: You pay taxes on money BEFORE putting it in
  • Grows tax-free: Your investments grow and you NEVER pay taxes again!
  • Withdrawals: 100% tax-free in retirement üéâ
  • Income limits: You can't contribute if you make too much (see below)
  • Can withdraw contributions: You can take out what you put in anytime (without penalty!)
‚úÖ Best If:

‚Ä¢ You're young or early career
‚Ä¢ You expect to make more money later
‚Ä¢ You're in a lower tax bracket now
‚Ä¢ You want flexibility to access your money

üîµ Traditional IRA

  • Tax LATER: You get a tax deduction NOW when you contribute
  • Grows tax-deferred: No taxes while it grows
  • Withdrawals: Taxed as regular income in retirement
  • Income limits: Deduction may be limited if you have a 401k
  • Early withdrawal penalty: 10% penalty if you take money out before 59¬Ω
‚úÖ Best If:

‚Ä¢ You're in a high tax bracket now
‚Ä¢ You expect lower income in retirement
‚Ä¢ You want to reduce this year's taxes
‚Ä¢ You don't need access to the money early

üéØ Quick Decision Guide (Pick Yours!)

What's Your Situation?

üì± Young and early in your career? ‚Üí Roth
üí∞ Making under $100,000/year? ‚Üí Roth
üìà Making $150,000+ per year? ‚Üí Traditional
‚ùì Not sure what taxes will be later? ‚Üí Roth
üè† Need tax deduction this year? ‚Üí Traditional
üîì Might need to access money early? ‚Üí Roth
üí° When In Doubt, Choose ROTH!

For most people, especially those under 40 or making under $100K, Roth is usually the better choice. Tax-free growth is POWERFUL, and having the flexibility to access your contributions is a nice safety net.

üéÅ Roth IRA Secret Bonus!

üîì You Can Withdraw Contributions Anytime!

With a Roth IRA, you can take out the money YOU contributed (not the earnings/growth) at any time, for any reason, with NO penalty and NO taxes!

Example: You've contributed $10,000 over the years, and it's grown to $15,000. You can withdraw up to $10,000 anytime - penalty free!

This makes Roth a great "backup" emergency fund! (But try not to touch it if you can help it - let it grow!)

‚ö†Ô∏è Income Limits to Know
üî¥ Roth IRA Income Limits (2024)

Single filers: Full contribution if income under $146,000. Reduced contribution $146K-$161K. Can't contribute if over $161K.

Married filing jointly: Full contribution if income under $230,000. Reduced $230K-$240K. Can't contribute if over $240K.

If you make too much for Roth, use Traditional IRA or look into "Backdoor Roth" (ask a financial advisor).

üí° Pro Tips
üéØ IRA vs 401(k) - What Order?

1. First: Get your employer's full 401(k) match (free money!)

2. Then: Max out your Roth IRA ($7,000/year)

3. Finally: Go back and max out your 401(k) if you can
