What's an IRA?

An IRA (Individual Retirement Account) is a tax-advantaged account YOU open yourself, separate from any employer plan. You can contribute up to $7,000/year (2024) or $8,000 if you're 50+.

üü¢ Roth IRA

  • Tax now: Pay taxes on contributions today
  • Grows tax-free: Never taxed again!
  • Withdrawals: 100% tax-free in retirement
  • Income limit: Under ~$160K single / $240K married
  • Best if: You expect higher taxes later

üîµ Traditional IRA

  • Tax deduction: Lower your taxes today
  • Grows tax-deferred: No taxes until withdrawal
  • Withdrawals: Taxed as income in retirement
  • Income limit: Deduction phases out with 401k
  • Best if: You expect lower taxes later

üéØ Quick Decision Guide

Young and early career? ‚Üí Roth (you're likely in a low tax bracket now)
Making under $100K? ‚Üí Roth (tax-free growth is powerful)
Making $150K+? ‚Üí Traditional may save more in taxes now
Not sure? ‚Üí Roth is usually the safer choice for most people
üéÅ Roth Bonus: You can withdraw your contributions (not earnings) anytime without penalty. This makes Roth a great "backup" emergency fund.
