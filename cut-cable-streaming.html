üìã Your Cord-Cutting Checklist

‚úì
Step 1: List what you actually watch
For 2 weeks, write down every show you watch. You'll probably find you only watch 5-10 channels regularly!
‚úì
Step 2: Check if you need live TV
Do you watch sports, news, or shows as they air? If not, you may only need on-demand streaming services.
‚úì
Step 3: Get an antenna for local channels
A $20-40 antenna gives you ABC, CBS, NBC, FOX, and PBS for FREE in HD. Great for live sports and news!
‚úì
Step 4: Choose 1-2 paid streaming services
Pick services with content you'll actually watch. Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, Max, or Paramount+ are popular choices.
‚úì
Step 5: Add free streaming services
Tubi, Pluto TV, Peacock Free, and others have tons of free content with ads. Use these to fill gaps!
‚úì
Step 6: Consider a live TV streaming service (if needed)
Philo ($28), Sling ($40), or YouTube TV ($73) replace cable with live channels at a lower cost.
‚úì
Step 7: Call and cancel cable!
Return equipment to avoid fees. They may offer a retention deal - only take it if it's REALLY good.
‚úì
Step 8: Rotate services instead of keeping all
Subscribe to one service, binge what you want, cancel, move to next. No contracts!

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Cord-Cutting Mistakes

‚ùå Subscribing to too many services at once

Start with 1-2 services. Add more only if you actually watch them. 5+ services can cost more than cable!

‚ùå Forgetting about internet costs

You need good internet for streaming. Make sure your internet-only plan is cheaper than your current bundle.

‚ùå Not using free trials strategically

Most services offer 7-30 day free trials. Use them to test before committing!

‚ùå Keeping services you don't use

Review your subscriptions monthly. Cancel what you're not watching and rotate services instead!
