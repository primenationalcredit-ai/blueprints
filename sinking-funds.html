üéØ What You'll Achieve

Here's the truth: Most "emergencies" aren't really emergencies! Christmas comes every December - that's not a surprise. Your car will need repairs eventually - that's not a surprise. These are things you can PLAN for!

What are sinking funds? They're separate savings accounts for different goals. Instead of one big savings account, you have small "buckets" for each expense. One bucket for car repairs. One for Christmas. One for vacation. When the expense comes up, the money is ALREADY THERE!

The magic: When you KNOW you have $1,500 saved for car repairs, a flat tire is just an inconvenience - NOT a crisis. When you KNOW you have $800 saved for Christmas, December is stress-free. No credit cards. No panic. Just peace of mind!

Step 1: List ALL Your Predictable Expenses
Think about every big expense that happens during the year. Write them ALL down!
üìù Think About:
  • Annual events: Christmas, birthdays, anniversaries, back-to-school
  • Maintenance: Car repairs, oil changes, home repairs, appliances
  • Insurance: Car insurance, home insurance, renters insurance (if paid annually)
  • Medical: Dental cleanings, eye exams, prescriptions, copays
  • Fun stuff: Vacations, concerts, events, hobbies
  • Replacements: Phone, laptop, tires, mattress, furniture
  • Write down EVERYTHING you can think of!
‚úì
Step 2: Estimate the Cost for Each One
For each expense, figure out roughly how much it costs.
üí∞ How to Estimate:
  • Look at last year: How much did you spend on Christmas last year? On car repairs?
  • Check old bank statements: Look for these expenses in your history
  • Google average costs: "Average car repair cost" or "Average Christmas spending"
  • When in doubt, round UP: Better to have too much than not enough!
  • Be honest: Don't underestimate to make yourself feel better
‚úì
Step 3: Pick Your Top 2-3 Funds to Start
Don't try to save for everything at once! Start with 2-3 that matter most.
üéØ How to Choose:
  • What caused stress last year? Car broke down? Christmas debt? Start there!
  • What's coming soonest? If summer vacation is in 4 months, prioritize that
  • What's biggest? Big expenses cause the most stress - tackle those first
  • Be realistic: If you can only save $200/month total, split between 2-3 funds
  • You can add more funds later! Start small, build the habit, then expand

üí° Pro Tips for Sinking Fund Success
üéØ The Golden Rule: Don't Borrow Between Funds!
If your car fund has $1,000 but you want to use it for Christmas gifts - DON'T! Each fund has ONE purpose. Mixing them defeats the whole point. Discipline now = stress-free later!

üí∞ Start Small - Even $25/Month Helps!
Don't stress if you can't save big amounts right now. Even $25/month to a car fund = $300/year. That covers an oil change and minor repairs! Something is ALWAYS better than nothing.
