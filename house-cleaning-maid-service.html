üí° Why Cleaning is a Great Business

Here's why house cleaning is one of the best side hustles:

The benefits:

  • Always in demand - Busy families, professionals, and seniors need help
  • Low startup cost - You probably already have most supplies
  • Recurring income - Same clients pay you every week or two
  • Flexible schedule - You choose which days and times to work
  • Cash flow is fast - Get paid same day or within a week
  • Easy to scale - Add more clients as you build reputation
  • No degree needed - Just a good work ethic and attention to detail

üéí What You Need to Start

Start with basics - you can upgrade as you grow!

  • Basic cleaning supplies
    All-purpose cleaner, glass cleaner, toilet bowl cleaner, microfiber cloths, sponges, scrub brushes.

    Recommended products:

    • All-purpose: Fabuloso, Mr. Clean, or make your own (vinegar + water)
    • Glass: Windex or vinegar + water in spray bottle
    • Bathroom: Scrubbing Bubbles, Lysol toilet cleaner
    • Cloths: Get a 20-pack of microfiber cloths ($10-15)
    üíµ $30-50 Easy
  • Vacuum cleaner
    If you have one, great! If not, many clients have their own you can use. A lightweight stick vacuum is ideal for travel.
    üíµ $50-150 (or use client's) Easy
  • Mop or Swiffer
    A Swiffer WetJet or traditional mop. Swiffer is easier to transport between homes.
    üíµ $25-40 Easy
  • Caddy or bucket to carry supplies
    Keep everything organized! A cleaning caddy from Dollar Tree works great.
    üíµ $5-15 Easy
  • Rubber gloves
    Protect your hands! Get a few pairs of reusable rubber gloves.
    üíµ $5 Easy

üí° Pricing tips:

  • First clean is always more - Charge "deep clean" rate for first visit
  • Recurring discount - Offer 10-15% off for weekly/bi-weekly clients
  • Hourly minimum - Never go below $25/hour for your time
  • Add-ons: Inside fridge ($25), inside oven ($25), laundry ($20)

üìã Step-by-Step: Getting Started

Follow these steps to get your first paying clients!

  • Step 1: Practice your cleaning system
    Clean your own home (or a friend's) using a consistent process. Time yourself! You need to know how long things take.

    Efficient cleaning order:

    • 1. Declutter & pick up (not your job, but clear surfaces)
    • 2. Dust top to bottom (ceiling fans, shelves, furniture)
    • 3. Clean bathrooms (toilets, sinks, showers, mirrors)
    • 4. Clean kitchen (counters, appliances, sink)
    • 5. Vacuum all floors
    • 6. Mop hard floors
    • 7. Final walkthrough
    ‚è±Ô∏è 2-3 hours Easy
  • Step 2: Set your prices and services
    Decide what you'll charge based on the pricing guide above. Write down exactly what's included in a "standard clean."
    ‚è±Ô∏è 30 minutes Easy
  • Step 3: Do 2-3 cleanings for friends/family at a discount
    Offer 50% off to people you know in exchange for honest feedback and a review. This builds your confidence and gets testimonials!
    ‚è±Ô∏è 1-2 days Important
  • Step 4: Create a simple booking process
    How will people book you? Phone/text is easiest to start. Get a Google Voice number if you want a separate business line.
    ‚è±Ô∏è 15 minutes Easy
  • Step 5: Post and start marketing (see below!)
    Time to find clients! Post on Nextdoor, Facebook groups, and tell everyone you know.
    ‚è±Ô∏è 1 hour Easy
  • Step 6: Do your first paid cleaning!
    Show up on time, do amazing work, and ask for a review. One great job leads to referrals!
