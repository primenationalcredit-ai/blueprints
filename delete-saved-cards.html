Step 1: Remove Cards from Amazon (The Big One!)
Amazon is the #1 impulse buying site for most people. Let's start here!
üì¶ How to Remove Cards from Amazon:
On Your Computer:
  1. Go to Amazon.com and sign in
  2. Click "Account & Lists" in the top right corner
  3. Click "Your Account"
  4. Find "Payment options" or "Manage payment methods" and click it
  5. You'll see all your saved cards listed
  6. For EACH card, click "Remove" or the trash can icon
  7. Confirm when it asks "Are you sure?"
  8. Done! You'll have to manually enter your card for future purchases
On the Amazon App:
  1. Open the Amazon app
  2. Tap the three lines (‚ò∞) menu icon
  3. Tap "Account"
  4. Tap "Manage payment methods"
  5. Tap on each card, then tap "Remove"

Step 2: Remove Cards from Target, Walmart, and Other Stores
Retail sites make it SO easy to buy things. Let's remove that temptation!
üõí How to Remove Cards from Retail Sites:
Target:
  1. Go to Target.com or open the Target app
  2. Sign in to your account
  3. Click/tap your name or profile icon
  4. Go to "Settings" then "Payment"
  5. Click "Remove" next to each saved card
Walmart:
  1. Go to Walmart.com or open the Walmart app
  2. Sign in and go to "Account"
  3. Find "Wallet" or "Payment Methods"
  4. Delete each saved card
Other Retail Sites (Same Process):
  1. Sign in to the website or app
  2. Go to "Account" or "Settings"
  3. Find "Payment Methods" or "Wallet"
  4. Delete all cards
  5. Repeat for: Best Buy, Home Depot, Lowes, Nike, Shein, Wayfair, Etsy, any store you shop at!

üçî How to Remove Cards from Food Apps:

üí° The Low-Limit Card Trick

üéØ Pro Strategy: Use a Low-Limit Card

If you MUST keep a card saved somewhere for emergencies, use a card with a LOW credit limit (like $300-500). This caps your potential impulse damage! You physically CAN'T spend more than the limit.

Many banks let you request a lower credit limit. Call and ask! Or use a prepaid debit card with only a small amount loaded.
