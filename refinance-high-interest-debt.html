üìã Your Debt Refinancing Checklist

‚úì
List all your debts with balances and interest rates
Write down every credit card, store card, and loan with its balance, APR, and minimum payment. This is your starting point.
‚úì
Check your credit score
Your credit score determines what rates you'll qualify for. Check free at Credit Karma, Credit Sesame, or your bank.
‚úì
Choose your best refinancing option
0% balance transfer (if you can pay off in promo period), personal loan (fixed payment), or other options below.
‚úì
Compare offers from multiple lenders
Get pre-qualified (soft pull, no credit impact) from at least 3-5 lenders to find the best rate.
‚úì
Calculate total cost including fees
Balance transfers have 3-5% fees. Personal loans may have origination fees. Factor these into your decision!
‚úì
Apply and transfer/consolidate your debt
Once approved, transfer balances or use loan proceeds to pay off high-interest debt immediately.
‚úì
Set up autopay and create a payoff plan
Calculate how much you need to pay monthly to be debt-free by your target date. Automate it!
‚úì
Don't add new debt to paid-off cards!
This is crucial. Keep the cards open (for credit score) but don't use them while paying off the consolidated debt.

üí° Your Refinancing Options

0% Balance Transfer Card

0% for 12-21 months

Transfer high-interest credit card debt to a new card with 0% intro APR. Pay no interest during the promo period!

  • 0% interest for 12-21 months
  • Can save thousands in interest
  • Single payment to manage
  • 3-5% balance transfer fee
  • Need good/excellent credit (670+)
  • Rate jumps to 20%+ after promo
üí° Best for: High credit score, can pay off within promo period

Personal Loan

6-36% APR (fixed)

Borrow a lump sum to pay off all credit cards. Fixed rate and fixed monthly payment until it's paid off.

  • Fixed rate won't increase
  • Fixed payoff date (2-7 years)
  • One simple monthly payment
  • May have origination fee (1-8%)
  • Need fair-good credit for best rates
  • Longer term = more interest
üí° Best for: Want predictable payments, need more time to pay off

Credit Union Loan

6-18% APR typically

Credit unions often offer lower rates than banks, especially for members with less-than-perfect credit.

  • Often lower rates than banks
  • More flexible with credit issues
  • Personal service
  • Must be a member (easy to join)
  • May have smaller loan limits
  • Less tech-forward
üí° Best for: Fair credit, want personal service, already a member

Home Equity Loan/HELOC

8-10% APR typically

Borrow against your home equity. Very low rates, but your home is collateral!

  • Lowest rates available
  • Interest may be tax-deductible
  • Large amounts available
  • Your HOME is at risk!
  • Closing costs apply
  • Takes weeks to close
‚ö†Ô∏è Caution: Only if very disciplined - you could lose your home

Most lenders let you check your rate with a "soft pull" that doesn't affect your credit score. Pre-qualify with several lenders to compare before applying.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Warnings

üö´ Don't Run Up New Debt!

The biggest mistake: consolidating debt and then charging up the old cards again. You'll end up with DOUBLE the debt. Cut up the cards or freeze them!

üìÖ Watch the 0% Promo End Date

Balance transfer rates jump to 20%+ after the promo period. Set calendar reminders and have a plan to pay it off OR transfer again before it ends.

üí∞ Factor in ALL Fees

A 3% balance transfer fee on $10,000 = $300 upfront. An origination fee on a personal loan adds to your cost. Calculate total cost, not just the rate!
