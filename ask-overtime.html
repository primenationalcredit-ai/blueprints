Step 1: Check If Your Company Offers Overtime
Ask HR or check your employee handbook. Some salaried positions don't qualify. Know your hourly rate and overtime rate (usually 1.5x). If you're unsure, just ask‚Äîit's a normal question!
‚úì
Step 2: Decide How Many Hours You Want

‚úì
Step 3: Pick the Right Time to Ask
Choose a moment when your boss isn't stressed. After a positive interaction is ideal. Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons. During or right after busy periods is perfect!
‚úì
Step 4: Practice Your Script
Read the scripts above out loud. Pick the one that fits your style. Practice it a few times. The more natural it feels, the more confident you'll be when you ask!
‚úì
Step 5: Have the Conversation
Keep it simple and brief. Show enthusiasm for helping the team. Be flexible on timing‚Äîlet them know you're willing to work when it's needed. Smile and be positive!
‚úì
Step 6: If They Say Yes‚ÄîBe Reliable!
Show up on time, do great work, don't call out. Being reliable means you'll be the first one they call for extra hours. Thank your boss for the opportunity!
‚úì
Step 7: If They Say No‚ÄîAsk What Would Help
Don't give up! Ask: "What would need to change for overtime to be available?" or "Can you keep me in mind when opportunities come up?" Follow up in 1-2 weeks.

Step 8: Put That Extra Money to Work!
When you get extra hours, direct that money toward your goals‚Äîdebt payoff, emergency fund, credit repair, savings. Don't let lifestyle creep eat your overtime earnings!
