üìù Exactly What to Say (Word-for-Word Scripts!)

üìù Script #1: The Direct Ask (Best for Most Situations)

What YOU Say:
"Hey [Boss's name], do you have a quick minute? I wanted to ask about overtime opportunities. I'm looking to pick up some extra hours and I'm pretty flexible on when. Are there any extra shifts available, or any projects that need more coverage?"
Why this works: You're direct, you show flexibility, and you're asking an open question that's easy for them to answer.
üìù Script #2: Show Initiative (When Things Are Busy)
What YOU Say:
"I've noticed we've been really busy lately. I wanted to let you know - I'm happy to come in early, stay late, or work weekends if that would help the team. Just let me know what you need!"
Why this works: You're offering to solve THEIR problem. Bosses love employees who notice what needs to be done and volunteer!
üìù Script #3: Email Version (If You're Nervous)
Subject Line: Available for Extra Hours
Hi [Boss's name],

I wanted to let you know that I'm available and interested in picking up any overtime hours that become available. I'm flexible with my schedule and happy to help wherever needed.

Please keep me in mind for any extra shifts!

Thanks,
[Your name]
Why this works: Email gives you time to think, and your boss can respond when convenient. Good if face-to-face feels scary!
üìù Script #4: After They Say "We'll See" or "Not Right Now"
What YOU Say:
"I totally understand! Just wanted to put it on your radar. Would it be okay if I check back in a week or two? And if anything comes up before then, I'd love to be considered first!"
Why this works: You're being persistent without being pushy. You're giving them permission to think about it AND reminding them to keep you in mind.

üìã Your Step-by-Step Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Check If Your Job Offers Overtime
Before you ask, make sure overtime is even possible at your job!
üîç How to Find Out:
  • Check your employee handbook - Look for sections on "overtime," "extra hours," or "work hours"
  • Ask HR - Say: "I'm curious about the company's overtime policy. Is overtime available in my department?"
  • Ask a coworker who's been there longer - "Hey, does anyone ever work overtime here?"
  • Look at your pay stubs - If you've worked over 40 hours and got paid more, OT is available!
  • Salaried vs. Hourly: Hourly workers usually get overtime. Some salaried workers are "exempt" (no OT). Ask HR if unsure!
‚úì
Step 2: Pick the BEST Time This Week to Ask
Timing matters! Choose a moment when your boss is relaxed and approachable.
üìÖ Planning Your Ask:
  • Look at your boss's calendar (if you can see it) - find a light day
  • Pay attention this week - when does your boss seem happiest?
  • Best days: Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday
  • Best times: Mid-morning (after they've settled in) or early afternoon
  • Write it down: "I will ask on _________ at _________ o'clock"
  • If you have a regular 1-on-1: That's the PERFECT time to bring it up!
‚úì
Step 3: Practice the Script OUT LOUD
This sounds silly, but saying it out loud makes it SO much easier when it's real!
üó£Ô∏è How to Practice:
  • Pick ONE script from above that feels most natural to you
  • Say it out loud 3-5 times - in your car, shower, room, anywhere private!
  • Practice with a friend or family member if you can - have them pretend to be your boss
  • Think about follow-up questions they might ask: "When are you available?" "How many extra hours do you want?"
  • Know your answers: Be ready to say exactly WHEN you can work extra (mornings? evenings? weekends?)
  • The more you practice, the more confident you'll sound!
‚úì
Step 4: Have the Conversation!
This is it! Walk up to your boss and say what you practiced.
üí¨ During the Conversation:
  • Take a deep breath before you walk up. You've got this!
  • Start with: "Hey, do you have a quick minute?" (Respect their time!)
  • Use your script - it's okay if it's not word-for-word perfect
  • Be specific about your availability: "I can work Saturdays" or "I'm free after 5pm on weekdays"
  • Smile and be positive! Enthusiasm goes a long way
  • Listen to their response - take notes if they mention future opportunities
  • Thank them regardless of the answer: "Thanks for considering me!"
‚úì
Step 5: If They Say No - Ask This Follow-Up Question!
A "no" today doesn't mean "no" forever. Ask this magic question!
üîÆ The Magic Follow-Up Question:
  • Ask: "I understand! What would need to change for overtime to become available?"
  • This question: Shows you're serious, gets you information, and keeps the door open!
  • They might say: "When we're busier" ‚Üí Now you know to ask again during busy season!
  • They might say: "If someone quits" ‚Üí Now you know to jump on it when that happens!
  • They might say: "Budget reasons" ‚Üí Ask again after a few months
  • Also ask: "Is it okay if I check back in a couple weeks?" (Gets permission to follow up!)
‚úì
Step 6: Follow Up in 1-2 Weeks
If you don't hear back, a gentle reminder shows you're serious!
üìß How to Follow Up:
  • Set a calendar reminder for 7-10 days after your first ask
  • In person: "Hey, just wanted to check back about those extra hours. Any opportunities come up?"
  • By email: "Hi [Boss], just circling back on my availability for extra hours. Let me know if anything opens up!"
  • Don't follow up more than once a week - you don't want to be annoying!
  • Be patient but persistent - opportunities often come when you least expect them
  • If still no: Ask again in a month, or when you notice things getting busy

Step 7: When You Get Hours - Put That Money to Work!
You got overtime! Now make sure that extra money goes toward your goals!
üí∞ What to Do with Overtime Pay:

  • Pay down debt faster - Extra payments save you interest!
  • Build your emergency fund - Goal: 3-6 months of expenses
  • Invest in yourself - Certifications, courses, tools for a side business
  • Thank your boss when you get those extra hours - it'll help you get more!
