üìã Your Vending Machine Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Research and Plan
Understand the business: margins, time commitment, location requirements. Join vending Facebook groups. Watch YouTube videos from operators. Snacks typically have better margins than drinks!
‚úì
Step 2: Secure Your First Location
This is the hardest part! Approach apartment managers, small business owners, and property managers. Offer 10-20% commission or flat monthly fee ($25-75). Have a professional pitch ready!
‚úì
Step 3: Buy Your First Machine
Start with ONE used machine ($1,000-2,500). Check Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, UsedVending.com. Inspect thoroughly‚Äîtest every slot, check cooling, verify coin/card mechanisms work!
‚úì
Step 4: Add a Card Reader
Many people don't carry cash! Card readers (Nayax, Cantaloupe) cost $200-400 but can increase sales 20-30%. Monthly fees ~$15-25. Essential for modern vending!
‚úì
Step 5: Stock and Price Strategically
Buy from Costco, Sam's Club, or local distributors. Buy snacks at $0.30-0.50, sell for $1.25-1.75. Match or slightly beat nearby convenience stores. Stock what sells‚Äîtrack everything!
‚úì
Step 6: Establish Restocking Routine
Visit weekly or bi-weekly depending on volume. Track what sells fastest. Remove slow items. Keep machine clean and fully stocked‚Äîempty slots = lost sales!
‚úì
Step 7: Optimize Based on Data
Modern card readers show sales data! See what sells, adjust pricing, optimize product mix. Move underperforming machines to new locations. Data drives profit!
‚úì
Step 8: Scale to More Machines
Reinvest profits into more machines. Create efficient restocking routes (cluster geographically). 5-10 machines = real income. At 15-20+ machines, consider hiring help!
