üîß Equipment Needed

Starter Kit ($50-150)

  • Squeegee: 12" and 18" brass channel ($15-30 each)
  • Scrubber/T-bar: Applicator for soap solution ($15-25)
  • Bucket: 3-5 gallon ($10-15)
  • Cleaning solution: Dawn dish soap or pro solution ($5-15)
  • Microfiber towels: For detailing edges ($10-15)
  • Ladder: 6ft stepladder for most residential ($50-100)

Advanced Tools

  • Extension pole: Reach 2nd story from ground ($30-100)
  • Water-fed pole system: Pure water cleaning, no ladder needed ($200-1,000)
  • Razor scraper: For paint, stickers, hard water deposits ($10-15)
  • Screen cleaner: Quick screen washing tool ($15-25)

üéì Squeegee Technique

Master the Basics

  • Wet the window: Apply solution generously with scrubber
  • Scrub if needed: Work dirt loose, especially corners and edges
  • Squeegee stroke: Start at top, pull down in overlapping strokes
  • Wipe blade: After each stroke, wipe squeegee blade clean
  • Detail edges: Use microfiber to wipe edges and sill
  • Practice makes perfect: Speed comes with practice. Aim for streak-free.

  • Bundle services: Add gutter cleaning, pressure washing for bigger tickets
  • Screens extra: Always quote screens separately‚Äîeasy add-on revenue

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoid These Mistakes

  • Cleaning in direct sun: Solution dries too fast = streaks. Work in shade.
  • Dirty squeegee blade: Wipe blade between strokes. Dirty blade = streaks.
  • Skipping screens: Dirty screens = dirty windows right after rain
  • Underpricing: Know your value. Cheap prices = cheap customers.
  • No insurance: Get general liability ($300-500/yr). One broken window...
