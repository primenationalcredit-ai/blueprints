üìã Your Mobile Mechanic Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Build your mechanic skills
Focus on mobile-friendly repairs: oil changes, brakes, batteries, starters, alternators, belts. ASE certification helpful but not required. YouTube tutorials, hands-on practice!
‚úì
Step 2: Get your service vehicle
Van or pickup truck to carry tools and parts. Used is fine - just needs to be reliable! Organize it for efficiency. Professional lettering optional but helps!
‚úì
Step 3: Build your tool collection
Quality socket sets, wrenches, OBD2 scanner, floor jack and stands, work lights. Start with essentials, add specialty tools as needed. Buy quality - it lasts!
‚úì
Step 4: Get licensed and insured
Business license from your city/county. Liability insurance is essential ($300-600/year). Some areas require mechanic registration. Check local requirements!
‚úì
Step 5: Set up parts supply
Accounts with AutoZone, O'Reilly, NAPA for parts. Stock common items (oil, filters, batteries). Order specialty parts as needed. Wholesale pricing saves money!
‚úì
Step 6: Market your services
Google Business Profile (essential!), Nextdoor, Facebook. Join YourMechanic or Wrench for jobs. Flyers at office parks, apartments. Network!
‚úì
Step 7: Deliver quality, convenient service
Arrive on time, do quality work, clean up completely. Convenience is your selling point! Leave the area cleaner than you found it. Exceed expectations!
‚úì
Step 8: Scale with fleet accounts
Target small businesses with vehicle fleets - delivery services, contractors, real estate agents. Monthly maintenance contracts = recurring income. One fleet = steady monthly check!
