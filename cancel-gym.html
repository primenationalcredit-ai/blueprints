üìù How to Cancel Your Gym

‚úì

Step 1: Find Your Contract

Check email for signup confirmation. Know your terms: commitment period, cancellation notice required (usually 30 days), any cancellation fees.

‚úì

Step 2: Check for Cancel Online Option

Some gyms now allow online cancellation. Check your account settings first‚Äîit's the easiest route.

‚úì

Step 3: Send Certified Letter (If Required)

Many gyms require written notice. Send certified mail with return receipt. Keep a copy. Include: name, address, member ID, cancellation request, effective date.

‚úì

Step 4: Go In Person With Script

If you must cancel in person, use the scripts below. Stay calm and firm. Don't engage with retention offers.

‚úì

Step 5: Get Written Confirmation

Don't leave without written/email confirmation of cancellation date and that no further charges will occur.

‚úì

Step 6: Monitor Your Bank Account

Check next month's statement. If charged after cancellation, dispute with your bank and show your confirmation.

üìû Phone/In-Person Cancellation

You:
"Hi, I need to cancel my membership effective as soon as possible."
Them:
"Can I ask why you're canceling?"
You:
"I'm reducing expenses. I need to cancel."
Them:
"We can freeze your account for $10/month..."
You:
"No thank you. I want to fully cancel, not freeze. What do I need to do to complete this today?"
Them:
"There's a 30-day notice required..."
You:
"That's fine. I'm giving my 30-day notice now. Please confirm my cancellation date and final billing date in writing."

‚ö†Ô∏è Gym Cancellation Pitfalls

  • "You can only cancel in person": Check your contract‚Äîmany states require phone/mail options
  • "There's a $50 cancellation fee": Ask if it can be waived. Push back if not in contract.
  • "We can freeze instead": Freezing still costs money and the subscription resumes
  • No confirmation: NEVER leave without written proof of cancellation
  • Auto-renewal: Even after "cancellation," check bank statements next month

üí° Nuclear Option: Bank Block

If a gym refuses to cancel or keeps charging you:

  • Cancel the card on file (request a new card number)
  • File a dispute with your bank for any charges after your cancellation date
  • File complaint with your state Attorney General if needed
  • Leave reviews warning others about their practices
