üìã Your Energy Audit Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Check for air leaks
Use a lit candle near windows, doors, electrical outlets, and where pipes/wires enter walls. If the flame flickers, you have a leak. Mark all leaks with tape.
‚úì
Step 2: Test door and window seals
Close a dollar bill in each door and window. If you can pull it out easily, the seal is worn and needs weatherstripping. Also check for visible daylight around frames.
‚úì
Step 3: Inspect HVAC system
Check air filter (replace if dirty), feel for air escaping at duct joints, ensure vents aren't blocked by furniture. Check thermostat location (not in direct sunlight or near vents).
‚úì
Step 4: Audit water heating
Check water heater temperature (should be 120¬∞F, not 140¬∞F). Look for insulation on hot water pipes. Test showerhead flow (more than 1 gallon in 10 seconds = not efficient).
‚úì
Step 5: Check lighting
Count incandescent bulbs (each uses 90% more energy than LED). Note which lights are used most - these should be LED first. Check for lights left on in empty rooms.
‚úì
Step 6: Hunt for energy vampires
Walk through and count devices plugged in 24/7: TVs, gaming consoles, chargers, cable boxes, computers. These use power even when "off." Note where power strips would help.
‚úì
Step 7: Inspect appliances
Check fridge temp (38-42¬∞F), freezer (0-5¬∞F). Test fridge seal with dollar bill. Clean fridge coils. Check dryer lint trap AND exhaust vent for clogs.
‚úì
Step 8: Check attic/basement
Look for gaps where pipes/wires enter, check insulation depth (should be 10-14 inches), inspect ductwork for disconnections or visible damage.

üß™ How to Perform Key Tests

üïØÔ∏è Candle/Incense Test for Air Leaks

  1. Light a candle or incense stick
  2. Hold it near window frames, door frames, electrical outlets, and where pipes enter walls
  3. If the flame flickers or smoke moves horizontally, you have an air leak
  4. Mark the location with tape for later sealing

üíµ Dollar Bill Test for Door/Window Seals

  1. Open the door or window
  2. Place a dollar bill across the frame where it closes
  3. Close the door/window on the bill
  4. Try to pull the bill out - if it slides easily, seal is worn
  5. Test multiple spots around each door/window

üöø Showerhead Flow Test

  1. Get a bucket and a timer
  2. Turn on shower to normal flow
  3. Hold bucket under showerhead for exactly 10 seconds
  4. Measure water collected
  5. If more than 1 gallon in 10 seconds, consider low-flow showerhead ($15-30 saves $70+/year)
