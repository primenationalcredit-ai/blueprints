üß† The SMART Goal Formula (Made Simple)

Every good goal has these 5 things. If yours is missing ANY of them, it will probably fail!

S
Specific
M
Measurable
A
Achievable
R
Relevant
T
Time-bound

S = SPECIFIC (What exactly do you want?)

Bad: "Save money" ‚Üí Save how much? For what? This is too vague!

Good: "Save $5,000 for an emergency fund"

Ask yourself: What EXACTLY do I want? Can I picture it clearly?

M = MEASURABLE (Can you track progress?)

Bad: "Save more money" ‚Üí How do you know when you've saved "more"?

Good: "Save $5,000" ‚Üí You can check: Am I at $1,000? $2,500? $5,000? Done!

Ask yourself: Can I put a NUMBER on this? Can I track it weekly?

A = ACHIEVABLE (Is it actually possible?)

Bad: "Save $50,000 in 3 months" on a $40,000 salary ‚Üí Impossible!

Good: "Save $5,000 in 12 months" on a $40,000 salary ‚Üí Challenging but doable!

Ask yourself: Can I actually DO this? Is the math realistic?

R = RELEVANT (Does it matter to YOU?)

Bad: "Save money because my mom said I should" ‚Üí You won't care enough to try

Good: "Save money so I never have to borrow from anyone again" ‚Üí This fires you up!

Ask yourself: WHY do I want this? Does thinking about it excite me?

T = TIME-BOUND (What's the deadline?)

Bad: "Save $5,000 someday" ‚Üí "Someday" never comes!

Good: "Save $5,000 by December 31st, 2025" ‚Üí Now you have urgency!

Ask yourself: WHEN exactly will I achieve this? What's my deadline?
