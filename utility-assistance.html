Find programs to help pay your utility bills

üéØ What You'll Achieve

There are dozens of programs that help pay utility bills - from federal LIHEAP to local nonprofits. Many people don't know these exist or think they won't qualify. This guide walks you through every major program, how to apply, and what you need. Even if one program doesn't work out, there are others to try!

Get $200-2,000+ in Assistance

LIHEAP alone distributes $4+ billion annually. Plus utility programs, nonprofits, and weatherization!

üìã Your Assistance Application Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Check LIHEAP eligibility and apply
Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program helps with heating/cooling bills. Income limits are usually 150% of poverty level. Apply through your local Community Action Agency. Funds can run out - apply early!
‚úì
Step 2: Contact your utility company directly
Most utilities have their own assistance programs, payment plans, and hardship rates. Call and ask: "What assistance programs do you offer?" They WANT to help - it's better than shutting off service.
‚úì
Step 3: Apply for Lifeline phone discount
$9.25/month off phone bill or free phone service if you qualify through Medicaid, SNAP, SSI, or income. That's $111/year savings. Apply at lifelinesupport.org.
‚úì
Step 4: Apply for ACP internet discount
Affordable Connectivity Program provides $30/month off internet. Same eligibility as Lifeline. Apply at affordableconnectivity.gov. Some ISPs offer free internet to ACP households!
‚úì
Step 5: Call 211 for local nonprofits
Dial 2-1-1 for local assistance. Salvation Army, Catholic Charities, St. Vincent de Paul, and local charities often help with utility bills. They can also refer you to other resources.
‚úì
Step 6: Apply for weatherization assistance
FREE home improvements (insulation, weather stripping, efficient appliances) that lower bills permanently. Worth $1,000-6,000. Same eligibility as LIHEAP. Apply through Community Action Agency.
‚úì
Step 7: Set up budget billing
Even without assistance, most utilities offer budget billing - pay the same amount every month based on your average. No more surprise high bills in summer/winter!
‚úì
Step 8: Set calendar reminder to reapply
Most programs require annual reapplication. Set a reminder for 30 days before your benefit period ends. LIHEAP usually opens applications in fall.

üí° Already on SNAP or Medicaid?

If you already receive SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, or TANF, you're automatically eligible for many programs! You may be able to use your benefits letter as proof without income verification.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Tips

Apply BEFORE You're in Crisis

Many programs have limited funds and waiting lists. Apply as soon as applications open (usually fall for LIHEAP) rather than waiting until you're behind on bills. Emergency assistance is harder to get!

üí° Don't Get Discouraged

If one program says no, try others! Eligibility varies by program. LIHEAP, utility company programs, and nonprofits all have different requirements. Keep trying until you find help.

üí° Shutoff Protection

Many states have protections against utility shutoffs for vulnerable populations (elderly, disabled, families with young children) during extreme weather. If you're at risk of shutoff, call your utility AND 211 immediately.
