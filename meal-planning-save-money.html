üìã Your Meal Planning Checklist

‚úì
Step 1: Check what you already have
Before planning, look in your fridge, freezer, and pantry. Plan meals around what needs to be used up first!
‚úì
Step 2: Check store sales and plan around them
Look at weekly ads. If chicken is on sale, plan 2-3 chicken meals. If ground beef is cheap, plan tacos and spaghetti.
‚úì
Step 3: Plan 5-7 dinners (leave room for leftovers!)
Don't plan every single meal. Leftovers become lunch. Leave 1-2 nights for using up what's in the fridge.
‚úì
Step 4: Plan meals with overlapping ingredients
If you buy cilantro for tacos, plan another meal that uses it (stir fry, rice bowl). No wasted ingredients!
‚úì
Step 5: Write your shopping list from your meal plan
Go through each recipe and write down ONLY what you need. Check pantry again so you don't double-buy.
‚úì
Step 6: Prep ingredients on Sunday (or your day off)
Wash and chop veggies, cook grains, portion meat. When dinner is half-prepped, you won't order takeout!
‚úì
Step 7: Post your meal plan where everyone can see it
Put it on the fridge! When everyone knows the plan, there's no "I didn't know what to make" excuses.
‚úì
Step 8: Have 3 "emergency" meals memorized
Pasta, eggs, quesadillas - know quick meals you can make with pantry staples when plans fall through.
