Step 1: Check Your Calendar

Which nights are busy? Plan quick/easy meals those days. Have a potluck? Don't buy for that night. Work backwards from your schedule.

âœ“

Step 2: Check What You Already Have

Look in fridge, freezer, and pantry. Build meals around what needs to be used up first. This alone prevents massive waste.

âœ“

Step 3: Check Store Sales

Look at weekly ads (most come out Wednesday). Plan meals around what's on sale. Chicken on sale? That's your protein this week.

âœ“

Step 4: Plan 5-6 Dinners (Not 7)

Leave room for leftovers or spontaneous plans. Planning every single meal leads to food waste.

âœ“

Step 5: Make Your Grocery List

List exactly what you need for your planned meals. Organize by store section. Stick to the list!

âœ“

Step 6: Shop Once Per Week

Every extra trip = extra impulse buys. One focused trip with a list saves money and time.

âœ“

Step 7: Post the Plan Visibly

Put your meal plan on the fridge. Everyone knows what's for dinner. No more "what should we eat?" conversations.

ğŸ’¡ Pro Tips for Success

  • Start simple: Plan just dinners first. Add breakfast/lunch once it's habit.
  • Be flexible: Swap days as needed. The plan is a guide, not a law.
  • Keep a "hits" list: Track meals your family loved. Rotate these regularly.
  • Prep on Sunday: Chop veggies, marinate meat. Makes weeknight cooking faster.
  • Involve the family: Let each person pick one meal. Increases buy-in.

âš ï¸ Common Meal Planning Mistakes

  • Too ambitious: Don't plan 7 new complex recipes. Mix easy and involved.
  • Ignoring schedules: Busy Tuesday? Don't plan a 2-hour meal.
  • No flexibility: Leave 1-2 nights open for leftovers or surprises.
  • Not checking inventory: You probably already have half the ingredients.
