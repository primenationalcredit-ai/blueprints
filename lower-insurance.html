üìã Your Insurance Savings Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Gather all current policies
Pull up auto, home/renters, and any other insurance. Note coverage levels, deductibles, and current premiums. You need this info to get accurate comparison quotes.
‚úì
Step 2: Get 5+ competing quotes
Use comparison sites like Policygenius, The Zebra, and Insurify. Also get direct quotes from GEICO, Progressive, State Farm. Request IDENTICAL coverage to compare apples-to-apples.
‚úì
Step 3: Ask about ALL possible discounts
Multi-policy bundling, good driver, low mileage, paid-in-full, paperless, autopay, good credit, homeowner, professional/alumni associations. Ask explicitly - they won't always apply them automatically!
‚úì
Step 4: Consider raising deductibles
Raising your deductible from $500 to $1,000 can cut premiums 15-30%. Only do this if you have an emergency fund to cover the higher deductible if needed.
‚úì
Step 5: Call current insurer with quotes
"I got a quote from [Company] for $[amount] less. Can you match or beat it?" Retention departments often have authority to offer discounts to keep you.
‚úì
Step 6: Review coverage for unnecessary items
Do you need rental car coverage if you have a second car? Roadside assistance if you have AAA? Collision on an old car worth less than $3,000? Remove what you don't need.
‚úì
Step 7: Switch or stay with better rate
If switching, time it with your renewal date. Set a calendar reminder to shop again in 12 months - rates change and your loyalty won't be rewarded!
‚úì
Step 8: Improve factors that affect rates
Improve credit score (big factor!), take defensive driving course, install anti-theft devices, maintain clean driving record. These reduce rates long-term.

üìû Calling Your Current Insurer
