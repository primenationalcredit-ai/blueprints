üìã Your Snack Savings Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Buy a reusable water bottle
This single purchase saves $2-3 per day! Get an insulated bottle that keeps drinks cold. Fill it at home every morning. Cost: $15-25 one-time. Savings: $50+/month!
‚úì
Step 2: Buy snacks in bulk
Hit Costco, Sam's Club, or the grocery store bulk section. Buy: granola bars (box of 48), nuts (big bag), pretzels, cheese sticks, fruit. Cost per week: ~$10. That's $2/day for ALL your snacks!
‚úì
Step 3: Get a small cooler bag
A $10-15 insulated lunch bag keeps snacks fresh all day. Pack it the night before - takes 2 minutes! Include an ice pack for cheese, yogurt, and fresh fruit.
‚úì
Step 4: Prep Sunday night for the week
Spend 15 minutes portioning snacks into small bags or containers for the week. Pre-cut veggies, portion nuts into bags, stack granola bars. Grab-and-go each morning!
‚úì
Step 5: Keep a desk drawer stash
Store non-perishable backups at work: granola bars, nuts, dried fruit, crackers. Never be desperate enough to hit the vending machine! Restock weekly.
‚úì
Step 6: Track your savings
For one week, write down every snack you WOULD have bought. Add up the cost. That's your monthly savings √ó 4! Transfer that amount to savings each month.

üí° Pro Tips for Snack Success

üí° The "Never Hungry" Rule

Vending machine purchases happen when you're HUNGRY and DESPERATE. By always having snacks on you, you remove the temptation entirely. Pack more than you think you need!

üí° Make It Easy

Put your cooler bag next to your keys. Pre-pack snacks in grab-and-go portions. The easier it is, the more likely you'll do it. If it takes more than 2 minutes, you won't stick with it.

üí° Bring Coffee Too!

A thermos of home coffee saves another $3-5/day. That's $60-100/month on top of snack savings! See our Coffee Savings playbook for more tips.

üí° Healthy = Cheaper

Fruit, veggies, and nuts are often CHEAPER than junk food snacks AND better for you. You'll save money AND feel better. Double win!
