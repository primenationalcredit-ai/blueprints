üéØ What is Wealth Building?

Wealth building is about making your money work FOR you instead of just working for money. It's the difference between trading hours for dollars forever versus creating assets that generate income whether you work or not.

üí° The Simple Truth About Wealth

There are only 3 ways to build wealth:

  1. Earn more - Increase your income through skills, business, or career growth
  2. Spend less - Keep more of what you earn (you've already done this!)
  3. Invest the difference - Put your money to work in assets that grow
üìç Where You Are Now: If you're reading this, you've likely completed Steps 1-6 of your Blueprint. You've reduced expenses, possibly increased income, built an emergency fund, and paid down debt. NOW it's time to think about growing wealth for the long term!

üõ§Ô∏è The 6 Wealth Building Paths

There's no single "right" way to build wealth. The best path depends on your skills, interests, time, and starting capital. Here are the main paths:

What it is: Investing in the stock market through special accounts that give you tax breaks. Your employer's 401(k) often includes FREE MONEY through matching!

üîë Key Concepts:

  • 401(k): Through your employer. Money comes out pre-tax. Many employers MATCH your contributions (free money!)
  • Traditional IRA: You open yourself. Tax deduction now, pay taxes when you withdraw in retirement
  • Roth IRA: You open yourself. Pay taxes now, withdrawals in retirement are TAX-FREE
  • Compound interest: Your earnings make earnings. $500/mo for 30 years at 8% = $745,000!
‚úÖ Pros
  • Tax advantages save thousands
  • Employer match = free money
  • Set it and forget it
  • Historically reliable returns

üéØ Best For: EVERYONE! This should be your foundation. At minimum, contribute enough to get your full employer match.

What it is: Instead of picking individual stocks (risky!), you buy a fund that owns a little piece of HUNDREDS of companies. When the overall market goes up, you go up.

üîë Key Concepts:

  • S&P 500 Index Fund: Owns the 500 biggest US companies. One purchase = instant diversification
  • Total Market Fund: Owns basically ALL publicly traded companies
  • Expense Ratio: The fee you pay. Look for under 0.20% (Vanguard, Fidelity, Schwab)
  • Dollar Cost Averaging: Invest the same amount regularly regardless of price
‚úÖ Pros
  • Very low fees
  • Instant diversification
  • No expertise needed
  • Beats most "expert" stock pickers
  • Can access anytime (taxable accounts)
‚ùå Cons
  • Market volatility (down years happen)
  • No control over which companies
  • Taxes on gains (unless in retirement account)
üéØ Best For: People who want to invest simply without spending time researching stocks. "Set it and forget it" investors.
  • I understand what an index fund is
  • I understand dollar cost averaging
  • I know to look for low expense ratios

What it is: Making money from property - either through rental income, property value appreciation, or both. You can start small (REITs) or go big (buying rental properties).

üîë Key Concepts:

  • REITs: Real Estate Investment Trusts - buy shares of real estate like stocks. Start with $100!
  • House Hacking: Live in one unit, rent out others. Your tenants pay your mortgage!
  • Rental Property: Buy property, rent it out, collect monthly cash flow
  • Cash Flow: Rent collected minus all expenses (mortgage, taxes, repairs, vacancy)
  • Appreciation: Property value increasing over time
‚úÖ Pros
  • Monthly cash flow (passive income)
  • Tax advantages (depreciation, deductions)
  • Leverage (use bank's money)
  • Tangible asset you can see/touch
  • Hedge against inflation
‚ùå Cons
  • Requires significant capital (or creativity)
  • Not passive - tenants, repairs, management
  • Illiquid (can't sell quickly)
  • Location dependent
  • Risk of bad tenants, vacancies
üéØ Best For: People willing to learn, hands-on investors, those with access to capital or willing to house hack.

  • I understand what cash flow means

üéØ Recommended Order

Not sure where to start? Here's our recommended order:

1 Get Your Employer 401(k) Match

If your employer matches contributions, this is FREE MONEY. A 100% instant return! Always do this first.

2 Max Out Roth IRA ($7,000/year)

Tax-free growth forever. Open at Fidelity, Vanguard, or Schwab. Invest in a total market index fund.

3 Increase 401(k) to 15% of Income

Work up to contributing 15% of your income to retirement accounts.

4 THEN Choose Additional Paths

Once the above is covered, explore real estate, business, dividends, or taxable brokerage investing based on your interests and goals.

üí° The Power of Focus

Don't try to do everything at once! Master ONE path before adding another. Spreading yourself too thin leads to mediocre results everywhere.
