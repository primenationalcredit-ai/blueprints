üìã Your Emergency Fund Action Plan

‚úì
Calculate your monthly expenses
Add up rent/mortgage, utilities, food, insurance, minimum debt payments, and essential transportation. This is your baseline.
‚úì
Set your initial goal: $1,000
Before building a full emergency fund, focus on saving your first $1,000. This protects you from most common emergencies.
‚úì
Open a high-yield savings account
Keep your emergency fund separate from checking! HYSAs earn 4-5% APY vs 0.01% at regular banks. That's free money!
‚úì
Set up automatic transfers
Automate a transfer to your emergency fund right after each payday. Even $25/week adds up to $1,300/year!
‚úì
Find extra money to boost savings
Tax refunds, bonuses, cash back rewards, selling stuff - put ALL windfalls into your emergency fund until it's full.
‚úì
Build to 3-6 months of expenses
After $1,000, keep going! Your full goal is 3-6 months of essential expenses. More if you're self-employed or single income.
‚úì
Only use it for TRUE emergencies
A sale at Target is not an emergency! Use this fund only for: job loss, medical emergencies, essential car/home repairs, or urgent travel.
‚úì
Replenish immediately after using
If you have to use your emergency fund, make rebuilding it your #1 priority until it's full again.
