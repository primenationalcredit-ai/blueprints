üìã Your Kids' Items Selling Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Do a room-by-room sweep
Go through kids' closets, playroom, garage, and storage. Pull out EVERYTHING that's outgrown, unused for 6+ months, or abandoned. Be ruthless - if they haven't touched it, sell it!
‚úì
Step 2: Sort into piles
Create 3 piles: (1) Good condition = SELL, (2) Stained/worn = DONATE, (3) Broken = TRASH. Be honest - stained items won't sell and waste your time.
‚úì
Step 3: Bundle clothes by size
"Boys 3T lot - 15 pieces" sells MUCH better than individual items. Bundle by size and season (summer lot, winter lot). Include variety: shirts, pants, pajamas.
‚úì
Step 4: List big ticket items separately
Strollers, car seats, cribs, and bikes deserve their own listings. Take 5-6 photos, include brand/model, note any wear. These items sell for $50-200+ each!
‚úì
Step 5: Take great photos
Lay clothes flat on a clean surface in good lighting. For toys, show them working if possible. Group bundled items together in one photo. Clear, bright photos = faster sales!
‚úì
Step 6: Price to sell FAST
Price at 15-25% of original retail for used items. Check what similar items sold for on Marketplace. Price slightly lower to sell fast - your goal is to clear the clutter and get cash!

üí° Pro Tips for Maximum Sales

üí° Branded Items = More Money

Nike, Carter's, Gap, Gymboree, Primary, Hanna Andersson - these brands sell for more. List brand names in your title! "Nike Boys 4T Lot" gets more clicks than "Boys Clothes."

üí° Time Your Sales Right

Sell winter clothes in September/October, summer clothes in March/April. Sell back-to-school items in July/August. Parents buy ahead of the season!

üí° The "Just Take It" Approach

For items that haven't sold in 2 weeks, drop the price 25% or bundle with other items. The goal is CASH, not maximizing every dollar. Donate what won't sell.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Safety Notes

Car Seat Rules

Only sell car seats that are: (1) Unexpired (check the label), (2) Never been in an accident, (3) Have no recalls. Include the manufacture date and expiration in your listing. When in doubt, recycle it - safety first!

Cribs & Sleep Products

Check for recalls on CPSC.gov before selling cribs, bassinets, or sleep products. Drop-side cribs are illegal to sell. Include model numbers so buyers can verify safety.
