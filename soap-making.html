üìã Your Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Learn soap making safely
Start with melt & pour (no lye handling). Watch YouTube tutorials from Royalty Soaps and Bramble Berry. If doing cold process, learn proper lye safety‚Äîgoggles, gloves, ventilation!
‚úì
Step 2: Source quality ingredients
Order from Bramble Berry or Wholesale Supplies Plus. Get base oils (olive, coconut, palm), lye, fragrances, and colorants. Buy in bulk as you scale for better margins!
‚úì
Step 3: Develop signature recipes
Create 3-5 signature scent combinations and designs. Lavender, citrus, and "clean" scents are bestsellers. Test on friends and family for feedback!
‚úì
Step 4: Brand and package professionally
Beautiful packaging sells soap! Create a logo with Canva, design labels (ingredients required by law!), and develop your brand story (natural, handmade, local).
‚úì
Step 5: Start selling at farmers markets
Farmers markets are perfect for testing! Get direct customer feedback, refine your products, build a following. Booth fees: $25-75/day typically.
‚úì
Step 6: Open your Etsy shop
Etsy has built-in traffic looking for handmade soap! Great photos are essential. Optimize titles and tags for search. Offer gift sets!
‚úì
Step 7: Approach local boutiques
Pitch wholesale accounts to local gift shops, boutiques, and spas. Wholesale = 50% of retail. Consistent orders, less work per sale!
‚úì
Step 8: Scale your soap business
Larger batch production, more wholesale accounts, expand product line (lotions, scrubs). Consider subscription boxes or private label for other brands!
