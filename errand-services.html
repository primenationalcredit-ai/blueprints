üìã Your Errand Business Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Define your services
Pick 4-6 errand types you'll offer. Start with grocery shopping, returns, post office, and wait-time services. Be specific about what you will and won't do.
‚úì
Step 2: Set your rates
Research local TaskRabbit rates. Start at $25/hr for basic errands, $35/hr for wait services. Set 1-hour minimum. Add mileage for delivery.
‚úì
Step 3: Sign up for TaskRabbit
Create profile, complete background check (~$25), set availability. Approval takes 3-5 days. Write a friendly, professional bio!
‚úì
Step 4: Post on Nextdoor
Create a recommendations post offering errand services. Include rates, availability, and what makes you reliable. It's free!
‚úì
Step 5: Accept your first jobs
Say yes to almost everything initially. Focus on getting 5-star reviews. Reputation is EVERYTHING on these platforms.
‚úì
Step 6: Over-deliver on service
Communicate proactively, send photo updates, be early. Text "On my way!" and "Task complete!" Ask for reviews after every job.
‚úì
Step 7: Build recurring clients
Offer weekly packages at slight discount. "Every Tuesday grocery run" = predictable income. Move loyal clients off-platform to keep more money.
‚úì
Step 8: Raise rates and expand
After 10+ five-star reviews, raise rates $5-10/hr. Add premium services like personal shopping. Position as "personal assistant" for higher rates.

üö® Common Pitfalls

  • Using your own money: Never pay for items yourself. Get payment or client's card upfront.
