üíµ Pricing Strategy

How Junk Removal Pricing Works

  • Volume-based: Price by truck space used (1/4 truck, 1/2 truck, full truck)
  • 1/8 truck load: $75-125 (single item minimum)
  • 1/4 truck load: $150-225
  • 1/2 truck load: $250-375
  • Full truck load: $400-600+
  • Labor add-on: +$50-100 for stairs, long carries, heavy items
  • Special items: +$25-75 for mattresses, TVs, appliances (disposal fees)

üí∞ Hidden Profit Streams

Make Money TWICE on Every Load

  • Resale: Furniture, electronics, tools on Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist
  • Scrap metal: Appliances, copper, aluminum at scrap yards ($50-200+ per load)
  • Donations: Tax deductions for donated items (track for year-end)
  • Electronics recycling: Some places pay for old computers, TVs
  • Construction materials: Lumber, cabinets, fixtures resell to flippers
  • Antiques/collectibles: Learn to spot valuable items people throw away

üîß Equipment Needed

Startup Requirements

  • Truck or trailer: Pickup truck minimum. Box truck or dump trailer ideal.
  • Dollies/hand trucks: For heavy items ($50-150)
  • Straps and blankets: Secure loads, protect items ($50-100)
  • Gloves and safety gear: Heavy duty ($30-50)
  • Tarps: Cover loads for transport ($20-40)
  • Basic tools: For disassembly ($50-100)

üí° Pro Tips

  • Take photos: Before/after for marketing. Happy customers = referrals.
  • Sort everything: Never dump what you can sell, scrap, or donate.
  • Partner with realtors: Estate cleanouts when houses sell. Recurring leads.
  • Same-day service: Charge premium (+50%) for same-day pickups.
  • Know dump schedules: Plan routes to hit dump before closing.
  • Build scrap relationships: Scrap yards pay more for regulars.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Warnings

  • Hazardous materials: Don't take paint, chemicals, asbestos. Know what's prohibited.
  • Heavy lifting: Use proper technique. Back injuries end careers.
  • Insurance: General liability essential. Damage to property happens.
  • Legal disposal: Illegal dumping = big fines. Use proper facilities.
  • Secure loads: Items flying off truck = accidents and liability.
