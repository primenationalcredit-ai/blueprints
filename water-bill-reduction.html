üìã Your Water Savings Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Check for leaks (biggest hidden waste!)
Check all faucets, toilets, and under sinks for drips. A single dripping faucet wastes 3,000+ gallons/year. Running toilets can waste 200+ gallons PER DAY!
‚úì
Step 2: Fix running toilets
Drop food coloring in the tank. If color appears in the bowl without flushing, you have a leak. Usually just needs a $5-10 flapper replacement - 15 minute DIY fix!
‚úì
Step 3: Install low-flow showerhead
Standard showerheads use 2.5 gal/min. Low-flow uses 1.5-2 gal/min with same water pressure feel. Saves 2,700+ gallons/year per person. Costs $15-30, 5-minute install.
‚úì
Step 4: Add faucet aerators
These $3-5 attachments reduce faucet flow from 2.2 gal/min to 1.0-1.5 gal/min. You won't notice the difference but your bill will!
‚úì
Step 5: Optimize toilet water use
If you don't have a low-flow toilet, put a sealed bottle of water or sand in the tank to reduce water per flush. Or upgrade to WaterSense toilet (uses 1.28 vs 1.6+ gallons).
‚úì
Step 6: Change your daily habits
Turn off water while brushing (saves 4 gal/brush), take shorter showers (2 min less = 5 gallons), only run full laundry/dishwasher loads.
‚úì
Step 7: Check outdoor water use (if applicable)
Water lawn in early morning (less evaporation), use mulch in gardens, check for hose/sprinkler leaks, consider drought-resistant plants.
‚úì
Step 8: Read your meter to track progress
Check water meter, then check again after 2 hours with no water use. If it changed, you have a hidden leak. Also compare monthly bills to see savings!

üîç How to Detect Leaks

üöΩ Toilet Dye Test

  1. Remove toilet tank lid
  2. Add 5-10 drops of food coloring to tank water
  3. Wait 15-30 minutes WITHOUT flushing
  4. Check the bowl - if color appeared, the flapper is leaking
  5. Replace flapper ($5-10 at hardware store)
üí° Meter Check for Hidden Leaks

1. Make sure all water is OFF inside and outside your home

2. Read your water meter (note the exact number)

3. Wait 1-2 hours without using any water

4. Read meter again - if it changed, you have a hidden leak!
