üéí What You Need to Start

You can start with a basic setup and upgrade as you grow!

  • Pressure washer (2,000-3,000 PSI)
    You can start with an electric one ($150-300) or go gas-powered ($300-500) for more power. Gas is better for driveways and concrete.

    Recommended starter models:

    • Budget: Sun Joe SPX3000 (electric, ~$180) - Good for decks, siding
    • Mid-range: Simpson MSH3125 (gas, ~$350) - Good all-around
    • Pro tip: Check Facebook Marketplace for used ones!
    üíµ $150-500 Key Investment
  • Surface cleaner attachment
    This is a GAME CHANGER for driveways and flat surfaces. It cleans 4x faster and leaves no streaks. Worth every penny!
    üíµ $50-150 Highly Recommended
  • 25-50 foot hose extension
    The hose that comes with the pressure washer is usually too short. You'll need extra length to reach around houses.
    üíµ $30-60 Easy
  • Degreaser and house wash soap
    Simple Green or Zep degreaser for driveways. House wash soap for siding. Available at Home Depot or Lowes.
    üíµ $20-40 Easy
  • Safety gear
    Safety glasses, closed-toe shoes, and ear protection (gas washers are loud). Never wear sandals!
    üíµ $20-30 Essential
