üìã Your Film Extra Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Take quality photos
You need current photos: headshot (face), full-body shot, maybe a casual/lifestyle shot. No filters! Casting wants to see the real you. Phone photos are fine if well-lit.
‚úì
Step 2: Register with casting agencies
Sign up with Central Casting (free, in-person registration in major cities), Casting Networks, Actors Access. Register with multiple agencies to maximize opportunities!
‚úì
Step 3: Complete your profile thoroughly
List ALL special skills: sports, dancing, languages, driving, military experience, period looks (can you look like the 1950s?), formal wear you own, uniforms. Details get you booked!
‚úì
Step 4: Check for work daily
Casting calls go out daily! Check your registered platforms every morning. Enable notifications. Be ready to respond quickly - jobs fill fast!
‚úì
Step 5: Accept your first booking
Say yes to early opportunities! Write down ALL details: date, time, location, what to wear, what to bring. Arrive early, bring snacks, book, phone charger.
‚úì
Step 6: Be professional on set
Arrive early, don't use phone during filming, stay quiet, follow PA directions, don't approach actors. Be easy to work with - ADs remember reliable extras!
‚úì
Step 7: Build relationships and get repeat work
Great extras get called back! ADs and casting directors remember faces. Be friendly, professional, flexible. Some extras work the same show for years!
‚úì
Step 8: Consider joining SAG-AFTRA
After enough work, you may qualify for union membership. Higher pay ($187+/day), benefits, protections. Research SAG-AFTRA eligibility requirements!
