1 Pick Your Sale Date

Choosing the right date can make or break your sale!

‚úÖ BEST Days:

2 Check Local Rules & Permits

Some cities require permits for garage sales. Don't skip this!

  • Googled "[your city] garage sale permit"
  • Called city hall if unsure (usually non-emergency line)
  • Got permit if required (usually $5-$20)
  • Checked HOA rules (if applicable)
  • Know sign placement rules (can't put on utility poles in most places)
üìû What to ask: "Do I need a permit for a residential garage sale? How many days can I have one? Are there sign restrictions?"

3 Start Collecting Items to Sell

Go room-by-room and gather EVERYTHING you want to sell. Be ruthless!

üè† Room-by-Room Checklist:

  • Bedroom: Clothes, shoes, accessories, old bedding, lamps, decor
  • Closets: Coats, bags, luggage, items you forgot existed
  • Kitchen: Duplicate items, unused appliances, dishes, cookware
  • Living Room: DVDs, books, games, decor, old electronics
  • Kids' Rooms: Outgrown toys, clothes, baby gear, books
  • Garage/Basement: Tools, sports equipment, holiday decor, boxes you never unpacked
  • Bathroom: Unused products, extra towels, old hair tools
  • Office: Old electronics, books, desk accessories
Ask family members if they want to contribute items! More stuff = more money. You can split profits or let them keep earnings from their items.

4 Clean & Prep Items

Clean items sell for 30-50% MORE than dirty ones!

  • Wipe down all hard surfaces
  • Wash all clothing items
  • Test all electronics (and note if they work!)
  • Replace batteries in items that need them
  • Gather all parts/accessories for items
  • Inflate sports balls, bike tires
  • Remove personal info from electronics
Put instruction manuals with items if you have them - people pay more when they see the manual!

11 How to Handle Customers

üëã Greeting Customers:

  • Smile and say "Hi! Let me know if you have questions!"
  • Don't hover - let them browse
  • Be available but not pushy

üí¨ Handling Negotiations:

People WILL try to haggle. This is normal and expected!

Sample negotiation:
Customer: "Will you take $5 for this $10 item?"
You: "How about $7?" (Meet in middle)
OR: "I can do $5 if you buy two items"
OR: "It's priced to sell - $10 is my best price" (for good items)
After noon, be more willing to negotiate! Getting SOMETHING is better than putting items back in storage.

üõ°Ô∏è Safety Tips:

  • Don't let strangers inside your house
  • Have a helper watch while you make change
  • Move large bills to a safe spot inside
