Step 1: Assess Your RV's Rental Potential
Research what similar RVs rent for in your area on Outdoorsy and RVshare. Consider age, condition, amenities, and sleeping capacity. Newer, well-maintained RVs with modern amenities command higher rates!
‚úì
Step 2: Prepare Your RV for Rental
Deep clean inside and out. Fix any issues. Stock with essentials (dishes, bedding, first aid). Take high-quality photos showing every angle. First impressions matter‚Äîmake it shine!
‚úì
Step 3: Create Your Listing
Sign up on Outdoorsy or RVshare. Write detailed description covering sleeping capacity, amenities, rules, and local highlights. Upload 15-20+ photos. Set competitive initial pricing to get first reviews!
‚úì
Step 4: Set Up Insurance & Policies
Platform insurance covers rentals ($1M+ liability typically). Set your policies: security deposit amount, mileage limits, pet policy, smoking rules. Clear policies prevent problems!
‚úì
Step 5: Create a Handoff Process
Develop a smooth pickup/dropoff routine. Create an RV guide with operating instructions, campground recommendations, emergency contacts. Consider offering delivery for extra fee!
‚úì
Step 6: Get Your First Booking
Price competitively to attract first renters. Respond to inquiries quickly. Be flexible with scheduling. First 5-star reviews are crucial for building trust and attracting more bookings!
‚úì
Step 7: Perfect the Guest Experience
Send welcome message before arrival. Be available for questions during trip. Do thorough inspection at return. Request reviews from happy renters. Great reviews = more bookings!

Step 8: Optimize & Maximize Earnings
Raise prices as reviews accumulate. Add premium add-ons (camping gear, delivery). List on multiple platforms. Block dates for your own use during off-peak times. Consider a second RV!

üí° Create an RV Binder

A detailed "How to Use Everything" binder prevents most renter questions. Include step-by-step guides for systems (water, electric, propane), local recommendations, and troubleshooting. Laminate for durability!
