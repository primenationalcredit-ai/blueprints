üí° Insider Tips

  • Shop perimeter first: Fresh foods on edges, processed (expensive) in middle
  • Look high and low: Expensive brands are at eye level. Cheaper options are on top/bottom shelves.
  • Check unit prices: Bigger isn't always cheaper. Compare price per ounce.
  • Shop alone: Kids and partners add 10-40% to your bill
  • Pay cash: Physical money makes spending more real. Easier to stick to budget.
  • Shop less often: Every trip = impulse buys. Weekly is better than multiple trips.

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoid These Traps

  • End caps: Items at aisle ends are rarely the best deals
  • BOGO on things you don't need: 50% off nothing is still nothing
  • Pre-made/pre-cut: You're paying for convenience, not food
  • Checkout snacks: Impulse zone. Keep eyes forward.
  • "Natural" or "organic" when unnecessary: Often 50%+ more for same nutrition
