üìã Step-by-Step: Reduce Your Bill

Do these in order for maximum savings!

  • Step 1: Track what you're actually spending
    Look at your last 3 months of grocery spending. You can't cut what you don't measure!
    ‚è±Ô∏è 15 minutes Easy
  • Step 2: Download cash back apps
    Install Ibotta, Fetch Rewards, and your store's app. Free money for scanning receipts!

    Must-have apps:

    • Ibotta - Cash back on specific items. $5-20/month
    • Fetch Rewards - Points for any receipt. $5-15/month
    • Store apps - Kroger, Walmart, Target all have digital coupons
    • Checkout 51 - More cash back offers
    ‚è±Ô∏è 10 minutes Easy money!
  • Step 3: Meal plan for the week
    Plan 5-7 dinners before you shop. This prevents "what's for dinner?" takeout and reduces food waste!

    Simple meal planning:

    • Check what you already have in fridge/pantry
    • Plan meals that use similar ingredients
    • Include 1-2 "leftover nights"
    • Make your shopping list FROM the meal plan
    ‚è±Ô∏è 15-20 minutes/week Saves $100+/month!
  • Step 4: Shop at discount grocers
    Aldi, Lidl, WinCo, and Grocery Outlet are 30-50% cheaper. Same food, way less money.

    Compare these prices to your store:

    • Milk: Aldi $2.50 vs. Regular store $4.00
    • Eggs: Aldi $2.00 vs. Regular store $4.50
    • Bread: Aldi $1.25 vs. Regular store $3.00
    • Ground beef: Aldi $4.50/lb vs. Regular store $6.50/lb
    ‚è±Ô∏è Try it once! Biggest single savings
  • Step 5: Buy store brands
    Store brands are often made by the same companies! Try them - if you don't like it, go back to name brand for that one item.
    ‚è±Ô∏è No extra time Saves 20-40%
  • Step 6: Check weekly ads before shopping
    Plan your meals around what's on sale. Chicken on sale? That's dinner this week!
    ‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes Easy
  • Step 7: Buy in bulk (wisely)
    Only buy bulk items you'll actually use before they expire. Great for: rice, pasta, frozen meat, toilet paper.
    ‚è±Ô∏è Varies Saves 20-30%
  • Step 8: Reduce food waste
    The average family wastes $1,500/year in thrown-out food! Use what you buy.

    Reduce waste tips:

    • First in, first out - use older items first
    • Freeze meat you won't use in 2 days
    • Make soup or stir-fry with veggies about to go bad
    • Actually eat your leftovers!
    ‚è±Ô∏è Ongoing habit Huge savings!
