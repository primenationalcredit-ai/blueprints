üìã Your Babysitting Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Get CPR/First Aid certified
Optional but HIGHLY valuable! Red Cross offers affordable classes ($50-100). Makes parents feel safer AND lets you charge $2-5 more per hour.
‚úì
Step 2: Babysit for family/friends first
Get experience and ask for references! Even informal sitting for nieces, nephews, or neighbors counts. This builds your confidence and testimonials.
‚úì
Step 3: Create profiles on Care.com and Sittercity
These platforms connect you with families. Use a great photo (smiling, friendly), write a warm bio, list ages you're comfortable with, mention CPR if certified.
‚úì
Step 4: Post on Nextdoor and Facebook mom groups
Local mom groups are GOLD for finding clients! Parents love finding sitters recommended by their community. Search "[Your Town] Moms" or "Parents of [Town]."
‚úì
Step 5: Tell EVERYONE you babysit
Your parents' friends, neighbors, people at church, coworkers with kids - everyone knows parents who need sitters! Word of mouth is powerful.
‚úì
Step 6: Nail your first few jobs
Be early, engage with the kids, follow all parent instructions, clean up before they return. First impressions matter! Text photos of the kids having fun (with permission).
‚úì
Step 7: Ask for referrals and reviews
After 2-3 successful sits, ask: "Do you know any other families who might need a sitter?" One happy family can lead to 5+ new clients!
‚úì
Step 8: Build regular clients
Weekly date night families are the best! Offer a slight discount for regulars. Build a roster of 3-5 families who call you first.
