üìã Your Home Insurance Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Review your current policy
Find your declarations page. Note your current premium, coverage amounts, and deductible. Know exactly what you're paying before you call.
‚úì
Step 2: Get 2-3 competitor quotes online
Spend 10 minutes getting quotes from Lemonade, Progressive, State Farm, or Geico online. These take 5 minutes each and give you leverage!
‚úì
Step 3: Call your current insurer with THE SCRIPT
Use the script below. Be polite but direct. Mention you've been a loyal customer and have competitive quotes.

üìû The Insurance Negotiation Script

YOU SAY:

"Hi, I've been a customer for [X years] and I'm doing my annual insurance review. I've received some competitive quotes from [Lemonade/Progressive/etc.] that are $[amount] less than what I'm paying now. I'd really like to stay with you - can you review my policy for any discounts I might be missing, or match their rate?"


THEN ASK:

"I'd also like to make sure I'm getting all available discounts. Can you check if I qualify for: bundle discount, claims-free discount, security system discount, autopay discount, and loyalty discount?"


IF THEY SAY NO:

"I understand. What would you recommend I do to lower my premium? Would raising my deductible help? What about adjusting my coverage limits?"

‚úì
Step 4: Ask about raising your deductible
Going from a $500 to $1,000 deductible can save 15-25% on your premium. From $1,000 to $2,500 saves even more. Only do this if you have emergency savings!
‚úì
Step 5: Bundle if you haven't already
Bundling home + auto with one company typically saves 15-25%. Ask your auto insurer for a home quote, and your home insurer for an auto quote!
‚úì
Step 6: Switch if the savings are significant
If competitors offer 20%+ savings and your current company won't match, switch! The process is easy - the new company handles everything.

üí° Pro Tips for Maximum Savings

üí° The Deductible Sweet Spot

Most experts recommend a $1,000-$2,500 deductible. You'll rarely file small claims (it raises your rates), so a higher deductible saves money without much real risk. Just make sure you have that amount in your emergency fund!

üí° Review Coverage Annually

Home values change, so does your stuff. Review your coverage every year at renewal. You might be over-insured on some things and under-insured on others.

üí° Ask About "Paid in Full" Discount

Paying your annual premium in one lump sum instead of monthly often saves 5-10%. If you can swing it, this is easy money!

‚ö†Ô∏è Watch Out For These Traps

Don't Sacrifice Coverage for Price

Saving $200/year means nothing if you're underinsured when disaster strikes. Make sure your dwelling coverage covers the full rebuild cost of your home (not the market value). Ask your agent to explain what each coverage does before reducing it.

Check the Company's Rating

A cheap policy from a company that won't pay claims is worthless. Check AM Best ratings - stick with companies rated A or higher. They're financially stable and will pay when you need them.
