Step 1: Identify your best subjects
What do you know well? What could you teach? Even if you're not an expert, you can tutor levels below yours!
‚úì
Step 2: Set up your tutoring space
Quiet room, good lighting, reliable internet, webcam. Professional background! Test your video and audio before sessions.
‚úì
Step 3: Sign up on 1-2 tutoring platforms
Wyzant and Tutor.com are good starting points. Fill out your profile completely! Great photo and detailed bio matter.
‚úì
Step 4: Price competitively to start
Start lower to build reviews, then raise rates. $20-30/hr is reasonable to start for most subjects. Test prep can start higher.
‚úì
Step 5: Prepare for your first session
Review the material, have examples ready, test your tech. Being prepared builds confidence and impresses students!
‚úì
Step 6: Do great sessions and get reviews
Be patient, be prepared, celebrate student wins! Ask students to leave reviews. Great reviews = higher rates and more students!
‚úì
Step 7: Raise your rates as you get reviews
After 5-10 good reviews, increase your rate by $5-10. Repeat as demand grows. Top tutors charge $60-100+/hour!
‚úì
Step 8: Build private clients
Platform clients become private clients (you keep 100%!). Ask for referrals. Word of mouth builds your private tutoring business.
