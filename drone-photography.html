üìã Your Drone Photography Action Plan

Step 1: Get FAA Part 107 Certified
Required for ALL commercial drone operations in the US. Study 10-20 hours, take the $175 FAA knowledge test at a testing center. 60 multiple choice questions, need 70% to pass. Free study materials from FAA and Pilot Institute. Valid for 2 years‚Äîrecertification is just an online test!
‚úì
Step 2: Buy Your Drone Equipment
Start with a DJI Mini 4 Pro ($759+) or Air 3 ($1,099+). Buy at least 2-3 batteries for back-to-back jobs. Get ND filters for video work. Budget $1,000-1,500 to start, upgrade as you earn. DJI Fly More Combos are the best value!
‚úì
Step 3: Get Drone Insurance
Liability insurance is essential for commercial work‚Äîmost clients require it. Options: SkyWatch (on-demand, $10-20/flight), Verifly (hourly/monthly), or annual policies ($500-1,000/year for $1M coverage). Hull insurance covers your drone if it crashes. Never fly commercially without coverage!
‚úì
Step 4: Practice Flying and Build Your Portfolio
Log at least 10-20 hours of flight time before taking paid jobs. Fly everywhere legal‚Äîparks, neighborhoods, scenic areas. Capture stunning sample shots and videos. Offer FREE shoots for friends' homes or local businesses to build your portfolio. You need proof of your skills!

üí° Pro Tips for Success

üí° Check Airspace Before EVERY Flight

Use the B4UFLY app or Aloft (formerly Kittyhawk). Many areas require LAANC authorization through these apps (takes seconds). Flying in restricted airspace without authorization = FAA fines up to $30,000. Always check, always comply!

üí° Weather Is Your Enemy (and Friend)

Wind over 20mph, rain, and snow ground your drone. BUT clients in a hurry will pay premium rates for pilots who can fly at the right weather window. Track weather closely and be ready to fly when conditions are good!

‚ö†Ô∏è Never Fly Over People or Moving Cars

FAA Part 107 rules are strict. No flying over crowds, no flying over moving vehicles (without a waiver), no flying beyond visual line of sight. Violating these rules risks your license, massive fines, and criminal charges. When in doubt, don't fly!
