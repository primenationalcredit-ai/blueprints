üìú The Rules (Read These Carefully!)

‚úÖ ALLOWED (These Are OK)

  • ‚úì Bills on autopay - If a bill automatically pays during your 48 hours, that's fine. Don't change your autopay.
  • ‚úì Gas for work ONLY - If you MUST drive to work and need gas, that's ok. But no "fun" driving!
  • ‚úì True emergencies - A real medical emergency or car breakdown. NOT "I really want coffee."
  • ‚úì Food at home - Eat anything you already have in your kitchen. Get creative!
  • ‚úì Free activities - Parks, walks, library, calling friends - anything that costs $0!

‚ùå NOT ALLOWED (No Exceptions!)

  • ‚úó Coffee shops - Make coffee at home. Yes, even your "special" latte.
  • ‚úó Restaurants / takeout - No DoorDash, no drive-thru, no "just grabbing lunch."
  • ‚úó Online shopping - No Amazon, no Target, no "just looking."
  • ‚úó Grocery store trips - You prepped food before the challenge. Use what you have!
  • ‚úó "But it's on sale!" - Nope! Sales will happen again. This is about breaking the habit.
  • ‚úó Convenience store stops - No snacks, no energy drinks, nothing!
‚ö†Ô∏è The #1 Mistake People Make

People say "I'll just buy this ONE thing, it doesn't count." IT ALL COUNTS! The whole point is to go 48 hours without ANY unnecessary spending. One "exception" leads to another. Stay strong!

üìã Your Step-by-Step Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Pick Your 48-Hour Window
Choose WHEN you'll do this challenge. Pick 2 days in a row where you don't have big plans.
üìÖ Here's Exactly What To Do:
  • Best choice: A weekend (Saturday morning ‚Üí Sunday night) works great for most people
  • Open your calendar app right now - Look at the next 2 weeks
  • Find a weekend where you don't have parties, events, or trips planned
  • Block it off! Write "ZERO SPEND CHALLENGE" on both days
  • Set a reminder for 2 days BEFORE to do your prep shopping
  • Pro tip: Starting Friday night at 6pm ‚Üí ending Sunday at 6pm also works great!
‚úì
Step 2: Do Your Pre-Challenge Grocery Run
Stock up on EVERYTHING you'll need BEFORE your 48 hours start. This is the key to success!
üõí Your Pre-Challenge Shopping List:
  • Breakfast foods: Eggs, bread, cereal, oatmeal, fruit - whatever you like for breakfast
  • Lunch foods: Sandwich stuff, leftovers ingredients, soup cans, salad stuff
  • Dinner foods: Plan 2-3 easy meals you can make at home. Write them down!
  • Snacks: Chips, fruit, nuts, popcorn - things to grab when bored
  • Drinks: Coffee (if you drink it!), tea, juice, whatever you normally buy at stores
  • Treats: Ice cream, cookies, candy - yes, buy these! It's easier to resist going out if you have treats at home
  • Do this shopping 1-2 days before your challenge starts!
‚úì
Step 3: Hide Your Wallet and Cards
Remove the temptation completely! If you can't easily pay, you can't easily spend.
üí≥ Here's Exactly What To Do:
  • Take ALL cards out of your wallet - credit cards, debit cards, everything
  • Put them somewhere inconvenient - a drawer, a shoebox in your closet, give them to a friend
  • Remove cash from your wallet too - put it in the same hiding spot
  • Keep ONLY your ID - in case you need it for emergencies
  • Remove cards from your phone - go to Apple Pay/Google Pay and disable them for 48 hours
  • The goal: Make it HARD to spend money, not impossible. If there's a real emergency, you can get your cards. But you won't be able to impulsively buy things!
‚úì
Step 4: Delete or Log Out of Shopping Apps
Amazon, Target, DoorDash - these apps make spending TOO easy. Remove the temptation!
üì± Apps to Delete or Log Out Of:
  • Amazon - Long press the app, tap "Delete" or drag to trash
  • Target - Delete it. You can re-download later if you really need it
  • Walmart - Same thing, delete it
  • DoorDash, UberEats, Grubhub - Delete ALL food delivery apps!
  • Instacart - Delete it. No grocery delivery during the challenge!
  • Any other store apps - Nike, Shein, Wayfair, whatever you use
  • If you can't delete: At minimum, log out of each app so you can't one-click buy
  • Remember: You can re-download these after 48 hours. This is temporary!
‚úì
Step 5: Plan Your FREE Activities
Boredom is the #1 spending trigger! Fill your 48 hours with fun things that cost $0.
üéâ Free Things You Can Do:
  • Get outside: Go for a walk, hike a trail, visit a park, sit outside and read
  • Library: Borrow books, movies, or just hang out in a quiet space (totally free!)
  • Home workout: YouTube has thousands of free workout videos
  • Call friends/family: When did you last have a long phone call? It's free!
  • Cook something new: Try a new recipe with ingredients you already have
  • Clean/organize: Declutter a closet, clean out your car, organize your photos
  • Free streaming: Watch shows you already have access to
  • Game night: Board games, card games with people in your house
  • Write down 3-5 specific activities you'll do BEFORE your challenge starts!
‚úì
Step 6: Tell Someone About Your Challenge
Accountability is POWERFUL! When someone else knows, you're more likely to succeed.
üì£ Who To Tell and What To Say:
  • Text a friend: "Hey! I'm doing a 48-hour no-spending challenge this weekend. Can you help keep me accountable?"
  • Tell your partner/roommate: "I'm challenging myself to spend zero dollars from Saturday morning to Sunday night. Please don't suggest we order food!"
  • Post on social media: "Starting a 48-hour zero-spend challenge tomorrow! Wish me luck! üí™"
  • Tell a family member: "Mom/Dad, I'm working on my finances. I'm going 48 hours without spending anything!"
  • Ask them to check in: "Can you text me tomorrow to see how I'm doing?"
  • Why this works: You won't want to admit you failed! Public commitment = higher success!
‚úì
Step 7: When You Get the Urge to Spend - Do THIS!
You WILL feel the urge to buy something. That's normal! Here's exactly what to do when it happens.
üß† When You Want to Buy Something:
  • STOP - Take a breath. Notice the urge. Don't act on it yet.
  • WRITE IT DOWN - Grab your phone notes or paper. Write WHAT you want to buy and WHY
  • WAIT 10 MINUTES - Set a timer. Do something else. The urge usually fades!
  • DRINK WATER - Sometimes we think we're bored or stressed, but we're actually thirsty
  • CHANGE YOUR ENVIRONMENT - Go to a different room, go outside, call someone
  • ASK YOURSELF: "Do I NEED this or do I WANT this? Will I still want it in 48 hours?"
  • KEEP A LIST - After 48 hours, look at your list. How many things do you still want?
  • CELEBRATE THE URGE! - Each time you resist, you're building money-saving muscles!
‚úì
Step 8: Complete Your 48 Hours - YOU CAN DO THIS!
Start your challenge and push through! After 48 hours, celebrate your win!
üèÜ During and After Your Challenge:
  • DURING: Stay busy! Use your planned free activities. Text your accountability buddy when it's hard.
  • WHEN IT'S HARD: Remember - it's only 48 hours. You can do ANYTHING for 48 hours!
  • AFTER: Calculate how much you saved! Check your bank - no transactions = money saved!
  • REVIEW YOUR LIST: Look at the things you "wanted to buy" - how many do you still want?
  • CELEBRATE: Tell your accountability buddy! Post about your success! Feel proud!
  • LEVEL UP: Consider making this weekly, or try a 72-hour or week-long challenge next!
  • TRANSFER SAVINGS: Move the money you would have spent to your savings account!
