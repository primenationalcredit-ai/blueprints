üìù How to Participate

‚úì

Step 1: Find Studies in Your Area

Use ClinicalTrials.gov, local research hospitals, and paid study websites. Search by location, compensation, and health status.

‚úì

Step 2: Check Eligibility Requirements

Each study has specific criteria: age, health conditions, medications, lifestyle. Read carefully before applying.

‚úì

Step 3: Complete Pre-Screening

Phone interview or online questionnaire. Be honest‚Äîthey'll verify everything during screening.

‚úì

Step 4: In-Person Screening Visit

Physical exam, blood work, medical history. Usually free and gives you health info. No obligation to continue.

‚úì

Step 5: Review & Sign Informed Consent

Read EVERYTHING. Understand risks, time commitment, compensation structure. Ask questions.

‚úì

Step 6: Complete the Study

Follow all instructions carefully. Show up on time. Complete all visits to receive full compensation.

‚úì

Step 7: Get Paid & Get Records

Payment usually within 30 days of completion. Request copy of your health screenings for your records.

üõ°Ô∏è Safety & Your Rights

What Protects You

  • IRB Approval: Every study must be approved by an Institutional Review Board
  • Informed Consent: You must understand all risks before agreeing
  • Right to Withdraw: You can leave ANY study at ANY time without penalty
  • Medical Monitoring: Doctors monitor your health throughout the study
  • Compensation for Injury: Most studies provide treatment for study-related injuries
  • Privacy Protection: HIPAA protects your medical information

üí° Maximize Your Opportunities

  • Sign up for multiple databases: More profiles = more invitations
  • Be a "healthy volunteer": Phase 1 studies pay best, need healthy people
  • Check university hospitals: Often have the most studies and best pay
  • Ask about referral bonuses: Many sites pay $50-100 for referring friends
  • Request health records: Free bloodwork and exams have value
  • Build relationships: Return participants often get first access to new studies

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Warnings

  • Never lie about health: Dangerous and disqualifies you from future studies
  • Read the informed consent: Understand ALL risks before signing
  • Washout periods: Many studies require 30+ days between participation
  • Report side effects: Tell researchers immediately if something feels wrong
  • Tax implications: Clinical trial payments ARE taxable income
  • Avoid sketchy sites: Stick to established research institutions
