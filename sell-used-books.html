üìã Your Book Selling Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Download scanning apps
Get Amazon Seller App (free, essential!) and BookScouter. These let you scan barcodes and instantly see what books sell for. Can't source without them!
‚úì
Step 2: Start with books you own
Practice by scanning books on your own shelves. You might be surprised what's valuable! Sell a few to learn the process before sourcing.
‚úì
Step 3: Find local sourcing spots
Google library book sales in your area (booksalefinder.com helps!). Map out thrift stores. Join estate sale notification lists. These are your gold mines!
‚úì
Step 4: Go sourcing - scan everything!
Hit library sales, thrift stores, estate sales. Scan every book - you'll be surprised what's valuable. Look for textbooks, technical books, niche non-fiction. Aim to pay $1-3 per book max.
‚úì
Step 5: Set up your selling account
Create Amazon Seller account (Individual is free, Professional is $39.99/mo for volume). Or start with eBay/book buyback sites if you want simpler.
‚úì
Step 6: List and ship your books
For Amazon FBA: list books, pack in boxes, ship to warehouse - they handle the rest! For Merchant Fulfilled: you ship each order yourself (more work, lower fees).
‚úì
Step 7: Track what sells and profit
Note which book types are most profitable for you. Some sellers specialize in textbooks, others in vintage. Double down on winners!
‚úì
Step 8: Scale up and reinvest
Reinvest profits into more inventory. Build relationships with library sale organizers. Consider online arbitrage (buying cheap online to sell higher). Grow your inventory!
