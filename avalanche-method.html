üéØ What You'll Achieve

The Debt Avalanche is a debt payoff strategy where you pay off debts from highest interest rate to lowest, regardless of balance size. This method saves you the most money on interest over time - potentially thousands of dollars! If you're disciplined and motivated by math, this is your method.

üí° The Math Principle

High-interest debt costs you the most money. Kill it first, and you'll pay less overall.

üí∞ The Interest Savings Are Real

Save $500 - $5,000+ in Interest

Depending on your debt load and interest rates, the avalanche method can save you serious money compared to other payoff strategies!

üìö How the Debt Avalanche Works

1

List ALL Your Debts by Interest Rate (Highest First)

Write down every debt except your mortgage. Order them by interest rate from highest to lowest - the rate is what matters here!

2

Make Minimum Payments on Everything

Pay the minimum on all debts to stay current and avoid late fees and credit damage.

3

Attack the HIGHEST Interest Rate Debt First

Every extra dollar goes to the debt costing you the most in interest. This is where you stop the bleeding!

4

Roll the Payment to the Next Highest Rate

When the highest-rate debt is paid off, take that entire payment and add it to the next highest rate debt.

5

Repeat Until Debt-Free! üéâ

Like an avalanche crushing everything in its path, your payment power grows while interest savings pile up!

üî¢ The Math Behind It

When you carry a $5,000 balance at 24.99% APR, you're paying roughly $104/month in interest alone! By attacking this debt first, you stop that interest from compounding and eating your money every single month.

‚öñÔ∏è Avalanche vs. Snowball: Quick Comparison

üèîÔ∏è Debt Avalanche (This Method)

  • Pay highest interest first
  • Saves the most money mathematically
  • Takes longer to see first debt paid off
  • Requires strong discipline
  • Best for: Math-driven, disciplined people

‚ùÑÔ∏è Debt Snowball

  • Pay smallest balance first
  • Quick wins = high motivation
  • See progress faster
  • May pay more in interest
  • Best for: People who need momentum
üí° Hybrid Approach

Some people start with the Avalanche to knock out their highest-interest debt, then switch to Snowball for the remaining debts. Use whatever keeps you going!
