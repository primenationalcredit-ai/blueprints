üéØ What You'll Achieve

The debt avalanche method attacks your highest-interest debt first, regardless of balance. This saves the most money in interest and gets you debt-free in the shortest time mathematically possible. It's the optimal strategy for rational payoff.

Maximum Savings
Pay the absolute least amount of interest

üìä How Avalanche Works

The Simple 4-Step Process:

  1. List all debts by interest rate (highest to lowest)
  2. Pay minimums on everything except the highest-rate debt
  3. Throw ALL extra money at the highest-rate debt
  4. When that's paid off, roll that payment to the next highest rate

üìù Your Avalanche Action Plan

‚úì

Step 1: List ALL Your Debts

Include credit cards, car loans, student loans, personal loans, medical debt‚Äîeverything. Get exact balances and interest rates.

‚úì

Step 2: Sort by Interest Rate (Highest First)

Put the highest APR at the top. This is your first target, regardless of balance size.

‚úì

Step 3: Determine Your Monthly Debt Budget

How much total can you put toward debt each month? All minimums + any extra you can afford.

‚úì

Step 4: Set Up Minimum Autopays

Automate minimum payments on all debts so you never miss one. Late fees hurt progress.

‚úì

Step 5: Attack Highest Rate Debt

Everything above the minimums goes to your highest-rate debt. Be aggressive‚Äîevery dollar counts.

‚úì

Step 6: Celebrate & Roll Payment

When #1 is paid off, take its full payment (min + extra) and add it to the next highest rate. The avalanche grows!

‚úì

Step 7: Repeat Until Debt-Free

Each payoff frees up more money for the next. By the end, you're throwing huge payments at the last debt.

‚öñÔ∏è Avalanche vs Snowball

üèîÔ∏è Avalanche (Highest Rate First)

  • Mathematically optimal
  • Pays least total interest
  • Gets debt-free fastest
  • Requires patience (big debts may take time)
  • Best if motivated by math/savings

‚õÑ Snowball (Smallest Balance First)

  • Quick early wins
  • Psychological momentum
  • Pays slightly more interest
  • Eliminates accounts faster
  • Best if motivated by quick victories

üí° Which Should You Choose?

Mathematically, avalanche always wins. But the best method is the one you'll stick with. If you need quick wins to stay motivated, snowball works. If you're disciplined and hate paying interest, avalanche is your method.

üí° Avalanche Acceleration Tips

  • Increase income: Side hustles, overtime, selling stuff‚Äîall goes to highest-rate debt
  • Refinance first: Lower the rate on your highest debt THEN avalanche
  • Cut expenses: Every dollar saved = dollar toward debt freedom
  • Use windfalls: Tax refunds, bonuses, gifts ‚Üí straight to the avalanche
  • Track progress: Watching the number drop keeps you motivated

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes

  • Skipping minimums: Always pay all minimums first‚Äîlate fees destroy progress
  • Adding new debt: Stop using credit cards while paying off
  • No emergency fund: Keep $1,000 buffer so emergencies don't derail you
  • Giving up: Big balances take time‚Äîtrust the math, stay consistent
