üìã Your Warranty Claim Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Find your proof of purchase
Locate the receipt, email confirmation, or credit card statement showing the purchase date. For electronics, find the serial number (usually on a sticker or in settings).
‚úì
Step 2: Check manufacturer warranty status
Visit the manufacturer's website and look up warranty status using serial number. Many have online warranty checkers. Note what's covered and the expiration date.
‚úì
Step 3: Check credit card extended warranty
If manufacturer warranty expired, check if you paid with a card that extends warranties. Chase, Amex, Citi, and many others add 1-2 years free. Check your card benefits!
‚úì
Step 4: Document the problem
Take photos/videos of the defect. Write a clear description of the issue: what's wrong, when it started, what you've tried. Be specific but concise.
‚úì
Step 5: Contact warranty support
Call or use online chat. Have your proof of purchase, serial number, and documentation ready. Be polite but firm. Ask specifically for repair, replacement, or refund.
‚úì
Step 6: Escalate if initially denied
If denied, ask to speak to a supervisor. Mention you'll file complaint with BBB, state AG, or FTC. Many companies authorize claims when customers persist politely but firmly.
‚úì
Step 7: Get confirmation in writing
Get a case number, confirmation email, and shipping label (if applicable). Document everything including date, time, rep name, and what was promised.
‚úì
Step 8: Follow up if no resolution
If they don't follow through, call back with your case number. Post on social media (companies respond to public complaints). File BBB complaint if needed.

üí° How to File Credit Card Warranty Claim

1. Call the benefits number on back of your card (not regular customer service)

2. Provide proof of purchase and manufacturer warranty

3. Get repair estimate or proof item is unrepairable

4. Submit claim with documentation

5. Receive reimbursement (usually statement credit)
