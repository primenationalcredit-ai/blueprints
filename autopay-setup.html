Why This Works

Late payments hurt your credit score, cost you money in fees, and add stress. Autopay eliminates all of that. Set it once, forget it, and your bills are always on time.

Bills to Put on Autopay

  • Rent/Mortgage - Most important! Late payments hurt credit
  • Car payment - Auto loans report to credit bureaus
  • Electric bill - Avoid disconnection fees
  • Gas/Heat bill - Keep your utilities on
  • Water bill - Usually small but easy to forget
  • Internet service - Often has autopay discount
  • Phone bill - Often has autopay discount
  • Car insurance - Usually offers autopay discount
  • Health insurance - Don't let this lapse!
  • Renter's/Home insurance - Protect your stuff

Credit Card Autopay Strategy

Option 1: Full Balance (Best)

If you can afford it, set autopay to pay the FULL balance. You'll never pay interest.

Option 2: Minimum Payment (Safety Net)

At minimum, set autopay for the minimum payment. This protects your credit score even if you forget.

How to Set Up Autopay

  • Log into each biller's website or app
  • Find "Payment Settings" or "Autopay"
  • Link your checking account (not credit card for bills)
  • Choose payment date (2-3 days after your payday is best)
  • Confirm and save the settings
  • Take a screenshot for your records

Autopay for Savings (Pay Yourself First)

  • Set up automatic transfer to Emergency Fund on payday
  • Start with what you can afford (even $25/paycheck)
  • Increase by $10-25 each month as you cut expenses
  • Treat savings like a bill that MUST be paid
üí° Pro Tip: Schedule all autopay for 2-3 days after your payday. This ensures money is in your account. Also set calendar reminders to review your bills monthly - you still want to catch errors!

After Setup - Safety Checks

  • Set up low balance alerts on your bank account ($500 minimum)
  • Set up payment confirmation notifications
  • Add all due dates to your calendar anyway (backup)
  • Review statements monthly to catch errors or rate increases
