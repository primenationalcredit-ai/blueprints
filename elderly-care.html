üìã Step-by-Step Action Plan

‚úì

Get trained and certified

CPR/First Aid certification ($50-100) is essential. Consider Home Health Aide (HHA) training or dementia/Alzheimer's care certification for credibility.

‚úì

Complete background check and TB test

Families require background checks. Many states require TB testing. Get these done upfront‚Äîthey build trust immediately.

‚úì

Get licensed and insured

Check state licensing requirements. Get general liability insurance ($300-600/year) and bonding ($100-300/year). This protects you and reassures families.

‚úì

Define your services and rates

Decide what you'll offer: companionship only, full personal care, or specialty (dementia, post-surgery). Set competitive rates based on your area.

‚úì

Find your first clients

Sign up on Care.com and CareLinx. Connect with senior centers, hospital discharge planners, churches, and post on Nextdoor. Word of mouth is powerful!

‚úì

Provide compassionate, reliable care

Show up on time, every time. Be patient, kind, and build genuine relationships. Families trust you with their loved ones‚Äîhonor that trust.

‚úì

Build recurring clients

Aim for 3-5 regular weekly clients for stable income. Get referrals from happy families. Consider specializing (dementia, post-surgery, etc.).

‚úì

Scale into an agency (optional)

Hire other caregivers with thorough vetting. Manage multiple clients. Contract with healthcare systems. Consider Medicaid waiver programs.

üí° Pro Tips

  • Specialize: Dementia care, post-surgery recovery, or hospice support commands higher rates
  • Document everything: Keep care logs for families and your protection
  • Communicate with family: Regular updates build trust and referrals
  • Self-care matters: Caregiving is emotional‚Äîtake breaks and set boundaries
  • Get more training: Alzheimer's Association offers free caregiver courses

‚ö†Ô∏è Watch Out For

  • Scope creep: Don't perform medical tasks you're not licensed for
  • No contract: Always have written agreements on duties and pay
  • Burnout: Set boundaries on hours and emotional involvement
  • Payment issues: Require payment weekly or use platform escrow
  • No insurance: One accident without coverage can bankrupt you
