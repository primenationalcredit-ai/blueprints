üìã Your Elderly Care Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Get CPR/First Aid certified
Essential for working with seniors. Red Cross or American Heart Association courses cost $50-100. Many clients won't hire you without this.
‚úì
Step 2: Consider additional training
Home Health Aide (HHA) courses, dementia/Alzheimer's training, and CNA certification boost credibility and rates. Check state requirements.
‚úì
Step 3: Get background check and TB test
Families want proof you're trustworthy. Get a background check through Care.com or your state. TB tests are often required - get one at urgent care ($20-50).
‚úì
Step 4: Get insured and bonded
General liability insurance ($300-800/year) protects you if something goes wrong. Bonding ($100-300/year) protects against theft claims. Professional liability adds more protection.
‚úì
Step 5: Define your services clearly
Write down exactly what you will and won't do. Create a service menu with pricing. Be clear about boundaries - this prevents misunderstandings.
‚úì
Step 6: Create profiles on care platforms
Care.com, CareLinx, and Caring.com connect you with families. Great photos, detailed bios, and certifications help you stand out.
‚úì
Step 7: Network with referral sources
Hospital discharge planners, senior centers, churches, and geriatric care managers all refer families. Introduce yourself and leave cards.
‚úì
Step 8: Provide excellent, compassionate care
Be reliable, patient, and kind. Families trust you with their loved ones - honor that trust! Great care leads to referrals and long-term clients.
