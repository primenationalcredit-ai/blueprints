üö´ Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Picking Too Many Goals

"I'll save for an emergency fund AND pay off debt AND invest AND save for a house!"

Fix: Pick ONE. Finish it. Then pick the next one.

Mistake #2: Being Too Vague

"I want to save more money" or "I should be better with money."

Fix: Specific dollar amount + specific deadline date.

Mistake #3: Not Making It Automatic

Trying to manually remember to transfer money each week.

Fix: Set up automatic transfers. Remove yourself from the equation.

Mistake #4: Giving Up After One Slip

"I missed a month, so I guess I failed. Why bother?"

Fix: One bad week doesn't ruin everything. Get back on track next week!

Mistake #5: Keeping It a Secret

Not telling anyone about your goal.

Fix: Tell at least one person. Accountability makes you 2x more likely to succeed!

üîÑ What to Do When You Slip Up

Because you WILL slip up. Everyone does. Here's how to recover:

Step 1: Don't Beat Yourself Up

Seriously. One bad week doesn't erase all your progress. You're human. It happens.

Step 2: Figure Out What Happened

Why did you miss your savings this week? Unexpected expense? Emotional spending? No specific reason?

Understanding WHY helps you prevent it next time.

Step 3: Make a Plan for Next Week

Can you save a little extra next week to catch up? Can you cut something else?

Even if you can't catch up, just get back to your regular amount.

Step 4: Keep Going

The ONLY way to fail is to completely give up. As long as you keep trying, you're winning!
