Step 1: Check Vehicle Requirements

‚úì

Step 3: Apply to Multiple Platforms

Sign up for Uber AND Lyft. Background checks take 3-7 days. Apply to both simultaneously.

Your personal insurance may not cover you. Get a rideshare endorsement ($15-30/month) or commercial policy.

‚úì

Step 5: Set Up Your Car

Phone mount, charger cables (for you AND passengers), water bottles, mints, aux cable. First impressions = tips.

First week: drive different times/areas to learn where demand is highest. Take notes on what works.

‚úì

Use Gridwise or Stride to track miles, earnings, expenses. You'll need this for taxes (save 25-30%).

üéØ Pro Driver Strategies

  • Stack apps: Run Uber + Lyft simultaneously. Accept the better ride, go offline on the other.
  • Chase surge smartly: Don't drive TO surge‚Äîit disappears. Be IN the area before surge starts.
  • Airport queue strategy: Worth it for long rides. Skip if wait is 45+ minutes.
  • Hit bonus targets: Weekly ride challenges can add $50-$200. Plan your week to hit them.
  • Destination mode: Use when heading home‚Äîget paid to drive where you're going anyway.
  • Multi-app with food delivery: Slow rideshare? Switch to Uber Eats/DoorDash between rides.

üí° 5-Star Rating Tips

  • Clean car daily: Vacuum, wipe surfaces, no smells. Ratings = more rides.
  • Greet by name: "Hi [Name], heading to [destination]?" shows you're prepared.
  • Read the room: Some want to chat, some don't. Follow their lead.
  • Offer amenities: Phone chargers, water, mints. Small things = big tips.
  • Smooth driving: No hard brakes, no aggressive moves. Comfort matters.
