üìã Your Utility Savings Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Lower your thermostat (heating/cooling)
Every degree saves 1-3% on heating/cooling. Set to 68¬∞F in winter, 78¬∞F in summer. Use a programmable thermostat to reduce when you're asleep or away.
‚úì
Step 2: Lower water heater to 120¬∞F
Most water heaters are set to 140¬∞F by default. Lowering to 120¬∞F saves $36-61/year and prevents scalding. Still hot enough for everything!
‚úì
Step 3: Switch to LED bulbs
LEDs use 75% less energy and last 25x longer than incandescents. Replace your 5 most-used bulbs first. Saves $75+/year on a typical home.
‚úì
Step 4: Unplug energy vampires
Devices on standby use power 24/7. Use power strips for TV/gaming areas and unplug when not in use. Saves $100-200/year for the average home.
‚úì
Step 5: Wash clothes in cold water
90% of washing machine energy goes to heating water. Modern detergents work great in cold! Saves $60-100/year.
‚úì
Step 6: Fix leaks and running toilets
A dripping faucet wastes 3,000+ gallons/year. A running toilet wastes 200 gallons/DAY! Usually just needs a $10 flapper replacement.
‚úì
Step 7: Seal air leaks around doors/windows
Use weatherstripping and caulk to seal gaps. A $20 investment can save $100+/year by keeping heated/cooled air inside.
‚úì
Step 8: Clean/replace HVAC filters monthly
Dirty filters make your system work 15% harder. Set a monthly reminder. Also clean fridge coils and dryer lint traps regularly.
