üîß Equipment Needed

Starter Kit ($200-500)

  • Pressure washer: 2,000-3,000 PSI electric ($150-300) or rent to start
  • Surface cleaner attachment: For driveways/flat surfaces ($50-100)
  • Hoses & nozzles: Various tips for different surfaces ($30-50)
  • Safety gear: Goggles, boots, gloves ($30-50)

Growth Equipment ($500-2,000+)

  • Gas pressure washer: 3,000-4,000 PSI for commercial work ($300-800)
  • Soft wash system: For houses and roofs ($200-500)
  • Water tank: For jobs without water access ($100-300)
  • Trailer setup: Mobile unit for efficiency

üí° Pro Tips

  • Before/after photos: Take them EVERY job. Post on social media. They sell for you.
  • Surface cleaner: Must-have for driveways. 10x faster than wand alone.
  • Upsell everything: "I can do your sidewalk for $50 more while I'm here."
  • Seasonal push: Spring cleaning = huge demand. Market hard in March-May.
  • Target HOAs: Get one customer, put door hangers on whole street.
  • Annual reminder: Email past customers each spring. "Time for your annual wash!"

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoid These Mistakes

  • Wrong pressure: Too much PSI damages wood, paint, soft surfaces. Learn first!
  • No test spot: Always test inconspicuous area before full job.
  • Skipping insurance: One broken window or damaged surface can cost you.
  • Underpricing: Know your worth. Cheap prices attract problem customers.
  • No contract: Written quote with scope protects you from disputes.
