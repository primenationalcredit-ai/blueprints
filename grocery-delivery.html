üí∞ Batch Selection Strategy

üéØ How to Cherry-Pick the Best Batches

  • Calculate $/hour: Estimate 45-60 seconds per item + drive time. Is the payout worth it?
  • Tip percentage: Look for batches where tip is $1+ per item minimum
  • Avoid no-tip orders: No tip upfront usually means no tip after. Skip them.
  • Double/triple batches: Only take if payout is 1.5-2x a single batch. Often not worth it.
  • Store matters: Know which stores are organized (fast) vs chaotic (slow)
  • Distance matters: Far deliveries eat time. Local = more batches/hour.

üí° 5-Star Rating Tips

  • Send intro message: "Hi [Name]! Starting your order now. I'll send photos of any replacements!"
  • Photo replacements: Don't just replace‚Äîsend a photo and ask. Customers love this.
  • Produce quality: Pick the best produce. Customers notice and rate accordingly.
  • Bag organization: Cold with cold, don't crush bread, separate chemicals from food.
  • Delivery photo: Clear photo of bags at door. Protects you from "missing order" claims.

‚ö†Ô∏è Mistakes to Avoid

  • Taking every batch: Low-paying batches kill your hourly. Be selective.
  • Double batches trap: Often pay less per order. Calculate before accepting.
  • Poor replacements: Wrong substitutions = bad ratings = worse batches
  • Ignoring messages: Customers who can't reach you = refunds = bad ratings
  • Melted ice cream: Keep cold stuff COLD. Bring a cooler with ice packs.
