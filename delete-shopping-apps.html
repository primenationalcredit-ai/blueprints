üéØ Dark Patterns

Shopping apps are designed by psychology experts to make you buy impulsively. One-click buying, countdown timers, "only 2 left!" - all manipulation.

üìã Your Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Delete Amazon app
The biggest temptation for most people. Long-press the app, tap "Delete" or drag to trash. You can still use amazon.com in your browser if needed.
‚úì
Step 2: Delete Target app
"Just going to check" becomes $200 every time. Delete it. If you need something from Target, drive there or use the website.
‚úì
Step 3: Delete Walmart and other retail apps
Walmart, Shein, Wayfair, Home Depot, any retail app. If you shop there regularly, you can use the website instead.
‚úì
Step 4: Delete food delivery apps
DoorDash, UberEats, Grubhub, Instacart. These apps make spending $30-50 on food feel effortless. Delete them!
‚úì
Step 5: Delete clothing/fashion apps
Nike, Zara, H&M, Nordstrom, any fashion apps. Browsing these is window shopping that leads to buying.
‚úì
Step 6: Tell someone you did this
Tell a friend, family member, or post on social media. Accountability helps! They can remind you if you're tempted to reinstall.
‚úì
Step 7: Set up Screen Time limits (optional)
Use Screen Time (iPhone) or Digital Wellbeing (Android) to block reinstalling or limit browser shopping sites.
‚úì
Step 8: Replace the habit
When you feel the urge to browse shopping apps, do something else: read, walk, call a friend, check your savings balance.
