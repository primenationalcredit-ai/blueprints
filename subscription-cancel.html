üìû The Universal Cancellation Script

This script works for almost any subscription. Stay calm, polite, and firm.

üìû When You Call to Cancel
You:
"Hi, I'd like to cancel my subscription, please."
Them:
"I'm sorry to hear that. Can I ask why you're canceling?"
You:
"I'm cutting expenses and I'm not using it enough to justify the cost."
Them:
"What if we offer you 50% off for the next 3 months?"
You:
"I appreciate that, but I'd still like to cancel. I can always resubscribe later if I need it."
Them:
"Are you sure? We have a pause option..."
You:
"I'm sure. Please proceed with the cancellation and email me confirmation."

üí° Key Phrases That Work

  • "I'd like to cancel" - Direct, clear, no ambiguity
  • "I can always resubscribe later" - Shuts down urgency tactics
  • "Please email me confirmation" - Creates paper trail
  • "I've made my decision" - Ends the negotiation

üõ°Ô∏è Common Retention Tactics & How to Beat Them

What They'll Say & Your Response

  • "Can I ask why you're canceling?"
    ‚Üí "I'm cutting expenses. Please proceed with cancellation."
  • "We can offer you X% off..."
    ‚Üí "I appreciate that, but I've made my decision."
  • "What if we pause your account instead?"
    ‚Üí "No, I want to fully cancel. Not pause."
  • "You'll lose all your [data/history/perks]..."
    ‚Üí "I understand. Please proceed."
  • "Let me transfer you to a specialist..."
    ‚Üí "I don't need a specialist. I need this cancelled."

    ‚Üí "I understand. I'm giving notice now. When is my final date?"

‚ö†Ô∏è Don't Fall for These Tricks

  • The Long Hold: They hope you'll hang up. Stay on the line.
  • Multiple Transfers: Each person tries to retain you. State upfront: "I've decided to cancel. I won't be accepting offers."
  • Verbal Confirmation Only: Always get email/written confirmation.
  • "Call Back Tomorrow": Demand to complete cancellation NOW.

üí∞ Script for Negotiating (If You Want to Keep)

If you DO want to keep a service but at a lower price:

The Negotiation Script

You:
"Hi, I'm thinking about canceling my subscription. The price has gotten too high for my budget. Is there anything you can do?"
Them:
"Let me see what I can offer you..."
You:
"I'd really like to stay, but I need a significant discount. What's the best you can do?"
Them:
"I can offer 25% off for 6 months."
You:
"Is that the best you can do? I was hoping for closer to 50%. Otherwise I'll need to cancel."
Them:
"Let me check with my supervisor... I can do 40% for 12 months."
You:
"That works. Please apply that and send me confirmation."

üí° Negotiation Tips

  • Call at the end of the month (quotas make reps more generous)
  • Mention competitors: "I saw [competitor] is offering..."
  • If first offer isn't good enough, politely reject and say you'll cancel
  • Ask: "Is that the best you can do?" at least once
  • Set a calendar reminder to call again before the discount ends
