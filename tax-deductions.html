üè† Homeowner Deductions

Mortgage Interest

Up to $750K loan

Interest on mortgage for primary home and one second home. Get Form 1098 from lender.

Who: Homeowners with mortgage

Property Taxes

Up to $10,000

State and local property taxes (SALT cap of $10K includes state income tax too).

Who: Property owners

Home Office

$5/sq ft up to 300 sq ft

Simplified method: $1,500 max. Must be exclusive, regular use for business.

Who: Self-employed, some remote workers

Energy Credits

Up to $3,200/year

Solar panels, heat pumps, insulation, windows, doors. 30% of cost for solar.

Who: Homeowners making energy improvements

üè• Medical & Health Deductions

Medical Expenses

Over 7.5% of AGI

Doctor visits, prescriptions, dental, vision, therapy, medical equipment. Only amount exceeding 7.5% of income.

Who: High medical expenses relative to income

HSA Contributions

$4,150 single / $8,300 family

Health Savings Account contributions are fully deductible. Triple tax advantage!

Who: High-deductible health plan participants

Health Insurance (Self-Employed)

100% of premiums

Self-employed can deduct health insurance premiums for self, spouse, dependents.

Who: Self-employed individuals

üéì Education Deductions & Credits

Student Loan Interest

Up to $2,500

Interest paid on qualified student loans. Available even without itemizing!

Who: Anyone paying student loans (income limits apply)

American Opportunity Credit

Up to $2,500/year

First 4 years of college. 40% refundable. Per student.

Who: Undergraduate students/parents

Lifetime Learning Credit

Up to $2,000/year

Any post-secondary education, grad school, professional courses.

Who: Students of any age

üíº Work & Business Deductions

Self-Employment Tax

50% deductible

Half of self-employment tax is deductible. Automatic on Schedule SE.

Who: Self-employed, freelancers, gig workers

Business Expenses

Varies

Supplies, software, equipment, marketing, professional services, mileage (67¬¢/mile in 2024).

Who: Business owners, self-employed

Retirement Contributions

Up to $23,000 (401k)

Traditional 401k, IRA ($7,000), SEP-IRA ($69,000), Solo 401k contributions.

Who: Anyone with earned income

üíù Charitable & Other Deductions

Charitable Donations

Up to 60% of AGI

Cash and property to qualified 501(c)(3) organizations. Keep receipts for $250+.

Who: Anyone who donates (itemizers)

State/Local Taxes (SALT)

Up to $10,000

State income tax OR sales tax + property tax. Combined cap of $10K.

Who: Itemizers in high-tax states

‚≠ê Tax Credits (Better Than Deductions!)

Credits reduce your tax bill dollar-for-dollar. A $1,000 credit saves $1,000 in taxes!

Child Tax Credit

$2,000 per child

Children under 17. Partially refundable ($1,700). Income limits apply.

Earned Income Tax Credit

Up to $7,430

Low-to-moderate income workers. Refundable! Many eligible don't claim it.

Child & Dependent Care Credit

Up to $3,000-$6,000

Childcare/daycare expenses while working. 20-35% of expenses.

Saver's Credit

Up to $1,000

Credit for retirement contributions. Low-moderate income. Often missed!

EV Tax Credit

Up to $7,500

New electric vehicles. $4,000 for used EVs. Income and price limits apply.

Premium Tax Credit

Varies

Health insurance through marketplace. Can get advance payments or claim at filing.
