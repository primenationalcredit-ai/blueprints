üìã Your Drone Services Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Get FAA Part 107 Certified
Required for ALL commercial drone operations. Study 10-20 hours using Pilot Institute or FAA materials. Pass the $175 knowledge test. This is your foundation‚Äîwithout it, you can't legally charge for drone work!
‚úì
Step 2: Invest in Professional Equipment
For inspection work, consider DJI Mavic 3 or Air 3 with good cameras. For mapping, you'll need software like DroneDeploy or Pix4D. Thermal cameras (DJI Mavic 3T) unlock roof and solar inspections. Budget $1,500-4,000 for a professional setup.
‚úì
Step 3: Get Commercial Drone Insurance
B2B clients require proof of insurance. Get $1M liability minimum. Annual policies run $500-1,500/year. SkyWatch and Verifly offer flexible options. Hull coverage protects your equipment. This is non-negotiable for commercial work!
‚úì
Step 4: Learn Specialized Software
Mapping requires DroneDeploy, Pix4D, or similar for orthomosaics and 3D models. Inspection reports need professional formatting. Learn to process and deliver data clients actually need‚Äîraw footage isn't enough for commercial clients!
‚úì
Step 5: Choose Your Niche and Build Portfolio
Pick 1-2 industries to focus on: roofing, construction, agriculture, or infrastructure. Do free or discounted jobs to build samples. Create professional case studies showing your deliverables. You need proof you can deliver value!
‚úì
Step 6: Land Your First Commercial Clients
Direct outreach works best for B2B. Call roofing companies, email construction project managers, visit agricultural co-ops. Offer a discounted pilot project to prove your value. One happy client leads to referrals in their industry!
‚úì
Step 7: Create Professional Deliverables
Commercial clients expect professional reports, not just photos. Create branded inspection reports, mapping deliverables with measurements, and clear documentation. Your reports should look like they came from a professional firm!
‚úì
Step 8: Scale with Recurring Contracts
Convert one-time clients into recurring accounts. Offer monthly construction progress packages. Propose seasonal agricultural monitoring. Recurring revenue provides stability‚Äîaim for 3-5 recurring contracts as your foundation!

üí° Pro Tips for Success

üí° Thermal = Premium Pricing

A thermal camera unlocks roof, solar, and electrical inspections. DJI Mavic 3T or similar adds $1,500-3,000 to your setup but lets you charge 2-3x more. Thermal finds problems invisible to regular cameras!

üí° Certifications Add Credibility

Industry certifications differentiate you. Thermography certification (Level 1) costs $1,500-2,500 but lets you do professional thermal reports. AUVSI TOP certification shows professionalism. Clients pay more for certified pilots!

‚ö†Ô∏è Know Your Airspace Limits

Commercial jobs often require LAANC authorization or waivers. Use Aloft or B4UFLY apps before every job. Some locations near airports may be impossible without planning. Check airspace BEFORE committing to a job!
