üìù Your Meal Prep System

‚úì

Step 1: Choose Your Prep Day

Most people prep on Sunday. Pick a 2-3 hour window when you're home. Put it on your calendar like an appointment.

‚úì

Step 2: Plan 3-4 Recipes

Start simple: 1 protein (chicken, ground beef), 1 grain (rice, pasta), 1 roasted veggie, and 1 soup or stew. Mix and match for variety.

‚úì

Step 3: Make Your Grocery List

List all ingredients needed. Check what you already have. Shop once for the whole week. Stick to the list!

‚úì

Step 4: Get Containers

Invest in good glass containers with compartments. You need 10-15 for a week of lunches and dinners.

‚úì

Step 5: Batch Cook Efficiently

Cook multiple things at once: oven roasting while stovetop simmers. Start with longest-cooking items first.

‚úì

Step 6: Portion Into Containers

Divide into single-serving containers while still warm. Label with contents and date. Refrigerate what you'll eat in 4-5 days, freeze the rest.

üí° Meal Prep Pro Tips

  • Cook proteins plain: Season differently throughout week for variety
  • Keep sauces separate: Add when reheating to avoid soggy food
  • Freeze half: If cooking for 1-2, freeze half to avoid food fatigue
  • Breakfast prep too: Overnight oats, egg muffins, smoothie packs
  • Theme nights: Taco Tuesday, Stir-fry Friday reduces decision fatigue
  • Repurpose leftovers: Monday's roast chicken becomes Wednesday's chicken salad

‚ö†Ô∏è Food Safety Rules

  • Refrigerated meals last 4-5 days max
  • Cool food before refrigerating (within 2 hours)
  • Freeze anything you won't eat within 4 days
  • Reheat to 165¬∞F internal temperature
  • When in doubt, throw it out!
