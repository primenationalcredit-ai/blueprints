Step 1: Check if your car qualifies
Turo accepts most cars 2008 or newer with under 130K miles. Go to turo.com and check their requirements for your specific vehicle.
‚úì
Step 2: Research your market
Search Turo for similar cars in your city. What are they charging? How many reviews do top hosts have? This tells you the opportunity.
‚úì
Step 3: Detail your car inside and out
First impressions matter! Clean thoroughly - vacuum, wipe down, wash exterior. A $20 detail can mean $20+ more per day in bookings.
‚úì
Step 4: Take amazing photos (20+)
Great photos are essential! Shoot in good natural lighting. All angles: front, back, sides, interior, trunk, dashboard, features. Clean backgrounds.
‚úì
Step 5: Create your listing
Write a detailed description highlighting features (Bluetooth, backup cam, gas mileage, etc.). Be specific about what makes your car great!
‚úì
Step 6: Set competitive pricing
Start 10-15% below similar cars to get your first reviews. Use Turo's pricing tools. Offer weekly/monthly discounts for more bookings.
‚úì
Step 7: Choose protection plan and handoff method
75% plan is the best balance. Decide: in-person handoffs or remote unlock (Turo Go/lockbox)? Both work well.
‚úì
Step 8: Get 5-star reviews, then raise prices
Go above and beyond for first guests! Once you have 5+ great reviews, raise your prices. Reviews = trust = higher rates.
