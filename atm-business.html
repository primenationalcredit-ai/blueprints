üìã Your ATM Business Action Plan

Step 1: Learn the ATM Business
Understand processing, compliance (ADA requirements), cash management, and maintenance. Watch YouTube channels from ATM operators. Join ATM Facebook groups. Know the business before investing!
‚úì
Step 2: Set Up Business Structure
Form an LLC for liability protection. Open a business bank account. Get business insurance. You'll need these for processor applications and to look professional to location owners!
‚úì
Step 3: Apply with ATM Processor
Processors connect your ATM to banking networks. Popular options: PAI, NationalLink, ATM Depot. They handle transactions and deposit your earnings. Apply before buying machines!
‚úì
Step 4: Find High-Traffic Locations
Approach bars, convenience stores, gas stations. Offer $0.25-0.75 per transaction OR flat monthly fee ($50-150). Get written agreements with exclusivity clauses. Location is everything!
‚úì
Step 5: Buy Your First ATM
Genmega and Hyosung are reliable brands. Buy new ($2,000-3,500) or refurbished ($1,200-2,000). Ensure ADA compliance. Consider professional installation for first machine!
‚úì
Step 6: Install and Configure
Secure installation (bolted down or weighted). Configure with your processor. Set surcharge amount (check local competition). Test everything before going live!
‚úì
Step 7: Establish Cash Loading Routine
Load cash weekly or bi-weekly based on volume. Track every dollar‚Äîcash management is critical! Consider armored car service as you scale (Loomis, Brinks) but expensive for small operators.
‚úì
Step 8: Scale to More ATMs
Reinvest profits into more machines. Create efficient loading routes. At 5-10+ machines, hire a loader or use armored service. Build relationships for better locations over time!

üí° Pro Tips & Warnings

üí° Bars = Best Locations

People at bars need cash for tips, cover charges, and cash-only purchases. They're less price-sensitive about surcharges. A good bar can do 200-400+ transactions monthly. Friday/Saturday nights are gold!

‚ö†Ô∏è Cash Security is Critical

You'll be carrying thousands in cash for loading. Vary your schedule, go during business hours, never load alone late at night. Consider insurance and proper safes. Security cameras at ATM locations help too!
