üßä Freeze Your Credit Cards

Put your cards somewhere inconvenient so you literally CAN'T impulse spend

üéØ What This Does For You

Here's the problem: When your credit card is in your wallet, spending is TOO easy. One tap, one swipe, and you've spent money you don't have. The card being convenient is what makes you use it!

By making your card physically hard to access, you create a "cooling off" period. Want to buy something? Now you have to go home, thaw out your card (or ask someone for it), and by then the impulse has passed. Most people find they don't actually want 80% of the things they would have bought!

$200-500+ Saved Monthly

That's $2,400-6,000 per year you're NOT putting on credit cards!

üß† Why This Works So Well

The Psychology of Friction

Research shows: Adding ANY friction to a purchase reduces impulse buying by up to 70%.

The harder something is to do, the less likely you'll do it on impulse.

If you have to wait even 10 minutes, most shopping urges pass completely.

Your future self will thank you when there's no credit card bill!

üßä The Ice Block

üîí The Lockbox

üì¶ The Hidden Drawer

üë• Give It Away
