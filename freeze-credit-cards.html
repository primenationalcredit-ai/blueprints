üßä Physical Freezing Methods

‚ùÑÔ∏è The Classic Ice Block

Put your cards in a container of water and freeze solid. To use them, you have to wait for it to thaw - giving you time to reconsider.

  • Use a plastic container (not a bag - too easy to cut open)
  • Fill halfway with water, add cards, freeze, then add more water and freeze again
  • Takes 2-4 hours to thaw completely
  • NEVER microwave (damages magnetic strip and chip)

üîí Lock in a Safe or Box

Put cards in a lock box and give the key to a trusted friend or family member.

  • They don't open it unless you explain why you need it
  • Having to justify a purchase stops impulse buys
  • Choose someone who will hold you accountable

üìç Keep Them at Home

Leave cards at home - only carry cash and your debit card with limited funds.

  • Store in a drawer you rarely open
  • Out of wallet = out of mind
  • You can still use for true emergencies at home

üì± Delete From Digital Wallets

Remove cards from Apple Pay, Google Pay, PayPal, and all shopping sites.

  • Go to Settings ‚Üí Wallet on your phone
  • Remove from Amazon, Target, and other saved accounts
  • Adding friction to online purchases = fewer impulse buys

üß† Mental Strategies

üí≠ The 48-Hour Rule

For any purchase over $50, wait 48 hours before buying. If you still want it after 2 days, it's not an impulse.

üéØ Know Your "Why"

Write down WHY you're freezing your cards and put it where you'll see it:

  • "I'm paying off $5,000 in debt by December"
  • "I'm saving for a house down payment"
  • "I don't want to pay $1,000/year in interest anymore"

üìä Track Every Urge

When you feel the urge to use your card, write down what you wanted to buy. After a week, review - you'll be surprised how much you didn't actually need.

üí° The Emergency Exception

Keep ONE card accessible (not frozen) for true emergencies only. Define what qualifies as an emergency BEFORE you need it:

  • ‚úÖ Car repair needed to get to work
  • ‚úÖ Medical emergency
  • ‚úÖ Urgent home repair (burst pipe, etc.)
  • ‚ùå Sale that "ends today"
  • ‚ùå Dinner out because you don't feel like cooking
  • ‚ùå Something you "deserve" after a hard week
