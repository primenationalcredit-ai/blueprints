ğŸ’ Building Trust With Seniors

Keys to Long-Term Relationships

  • Be patient: Seniors may move slowly, repeat stories, need extra time. That's okay.
  • Be reliable: Show up when you say you will. Every time. No exceptions.
  • Learn their preferences: Which grocery store, which brands, their routines.
  • Communicate with family: Keep adult children updated. They're often paying.
  • Notice changes: Health, mood, home condition. Alert family to concerns.
  • Be honest: About what you can do, pricing, timing. Trust is everything.
  • Provide receipts: Always show itemized costs. Transparency builds trust.

ğŸ’¡ Senior Service Pro Tips

  • Photo + name on flyers: Seniors want to see who's helping them
  • Start with one neighborhood: Efficiency + word spreads locally
  • Accept checks: Many seniors don't use apps. Checks are fine.
  • Keep emergency contacts: Know who to call for each client
  • Holiday visits: Check on clients during holidays. Means a lot.
  • Thank the family: A note to adult children goes a long way

âš ï¸ Important Boundaries

  • No medical care: You're not a nurse. Don't give medications or medical advice.
  • No financial management: Don't sign checks or manage accounts. Too much liability.
  • Document everything: Keep records of visits, purchases, time spent.
  • Report concerns: If you notice abuse, neglect, or health decline, tell family.
  • Insurance: Consider liability insurance as you grow. Protects you.
