Negotiate Medical Bills

Medical bills are NEGOTIABLE! Most people don't know this. A few phone calls could reduce your bill by 25-50% or set up a payment plan with no interest.

ü§î Why Medical Bills Are Negotiable

What hospitals don't advertise:

  • Prices are inflated - Hospital "list prices" are often 2-10x higher than what insurers actually pay
  • They expect negotiation - Hospitals have entire departments dedicated to working out payment
  • They'd rather get SOMETHING - A reduced payment now beats collections later
  • Financial assistance exists - Most hospitals have charity care programs they don't advertise
  • Bills often have errors - Studies show 80% of medical bills contain mistakes!

üìã Step-by-Step: Reduce Your Bill

  • Step 1: Request an itemized bill
    Call the billing department and ask for a detailed, itemized bill. The summary bill isn't enough - you need line items!

    What to ask for:

    • Itemized statement with procedure codes (CPT codes)
    • Diagnosis codes (ICD codes)
    • Individual charges for each item/service
    ‚è±Ô∏è 10-15 minutes Critical first step!
  • Step 2: Check for billing errors
    Review each line item. Look for duplicate charges, services you didn't receive, or incorrect codes.

    Common errors to look for:

    • Duplicate charges (same item billed twice)
    • Charges for services you didn't receive
    • Incorrect room charges (charged for private, got shared)
    • Medication errors (wrong dosage amounts)
    • "Unbundling" - charging separately for things that should be grouped
    ‚è±Ô∏è 30 minutes Often finds savings!
  • Step 3: Research fair prices
    Look up what Medicare pays for the same procedures. Your goal is to pay closer to that, not the inflated "list price."

    Where to check fair prices:

    • Healthcare Bluebook (healthcarebluebook.com) - Fair prices for procedures
    • Medicare.gov - What government pays for same services
    • FAIR Health (fairhealthconsumer.org) - Cost estimator
    ‚è±Ô∏è 15-20 minutes Gives you leverage!
  • Step 4: Ask about financial assistance
    Most hospitals have charity care or financial hardship programs. Ask! You might qualify for a significant reduction.
    ‚è±Ô∏è 15 minutes Often overlooked!
  • Step 5: Call and negotiate
    Call the billing department, be polite but firm. Ask for a reduction, payment plan, or both. See scripts below!
    ‚è±Ô∏è 20-30 minutes This is where savings happen!
  • Step 6: Get everything in writing
    Before paying, get the agreement in writing! Email confirmation works. Keep records of everything.
    ‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes Protect yourself!

üí¨ Scripts for Negotiating

Use these when you call the billing department. Be polite but firm!

üìù Script 1: Ask for a discount (cash pay)

"Hi, I received a bill for $[amount] for services on [date]. I'm calling to discuss payment options. I'd like to pay this bill, but the amount is more than I can afford. If I pay in full today, is there a cash-pay discount or prompt-pay discount available? I've seen that Medicare pays around $[lower amount] for this procedure, so I was hoping we could come to something closer to that."

üìù Script 2: Request financial hardship assistance

"I'm calling about bill #[number] for $[amount]. Honestly, this bill is causing financial hardship for my family. I want to pay what I owe, but I need help. Do you have any financial assistance programs or charity care I might qualify for? I'd be happy to provide income documentation if needed. I want to work something out rather than not pay at all."

üìù Script 3: Request a payment plan

"I'd like to set up a payment plan for this bill. What's the longest payment plan you offer? Is there any interest charged? I can afford $[amount] per month. Can we make that work? I want to pay this off - I just need some time."

üìù Script 4: Dispute an error

"I reviewed my itemized bill and I believe there's an error. I see a charge for [item] but I didn't receive that service / that appears to be a duplicate charge / that's not the correct code. Can you review this and correct the bill? I'll hold while you check, or you can call me back at [number]."
