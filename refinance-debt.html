üîÑ Refinancing Options

üí≥ Balance Transfer Card

0% APR for 12-21 months

Transfer credit card debt to a new card with 0% intro APR. Pay it off before promo ends.

Fee: 3-5% transfer fee

Credit needed: Good to Excellent (670+)

Best for: Credit card debt you can pay off in 12-21 months

üè¶ Personal Loan

7-15% APR (fixed)

Unsecured loan with fixed rate and payment. Consolidate multiple debts into one payment.

Fee: 0-6% origination fee

Credit needed: Fair to Excellent (580+)

Best for: Multiple debts, need fixed payoff date

üè† Home Equity Loan/HELOC

7-10% APR

Borrow against your home equity. Lower rates because it's secured by your house.

Fee: Closing costs 2-5%

Credit needed: Good (620+) + home equity

Best for: Large debt amounts, homeowners with equity

üìù How to Refinance

‚úì

Step 1: List All Your Debts

Write down every debt: balance, interest rate, minimum payment, type. You need the full picture to make smart decisions.

‚úì

Step 2: Check Your Credit Score

Your score determines what rates you'll qualify for. Check free at Credit Karma, Experian, or your bank. 670+ opens best options.

‚úì

Step 3: Compare Multiple Offers

Get quotes from 3-5 lenders. Many offer soft-pull prequalification that won't hurt your credit. Compare APR, fees, and terms.

‚úì

Step 4: Calculate Total Cost

Lower payment ‚â† lower cost. Calculate total interest paid over life of loan. Longer terms mean more interest.

‚úì

Step 5: Apply for Best Option

Once you've found the best deal, complete the full application. Have income docs ready (pay stubs, tax returns).

‚úì

Step 6: Pay Off Original Debt

Use new loan/card to pay off old debt immediately. Don't spend the money on other things!

‚úì

Step 7: Close or Freeze Old Accounts

Don't rack up new debt on paid-off cards. Consider closing or freezing to avoid temptation.

üí° Refinancing Pro Tips

  • Time it right: Wait until credit score improves for better rates
  • Shop within 14-45 days: Multiple hard inquiries for same loan type count as one
  • Watch the fees: 5% balance transfer fee on $10K = $500. Factor into savings calculation.
  • Don't extend term too much: Lower payment but more total interest if you stretch to 7 years
  • Automate payments: Many lenders give 0.25% rate discount for autopay

‚ö†Ô∏è Refinancing Pitfalls

  • Running up cards again: #1 mistake‚Äîdon't create new debt after consolidating
  • Losing federal loan benefits: Refinancing federal student loans = lose forgiveness, income-driven repayment
  • Secured vs unsecured: HELOC puts your house at risk if you can't pay
  • Balance transfer expiration: 0% ends and jumps to 20%+. Have a payoff plan.
  • Origination fees: Some personal loans charge 5-6% upfront. Calculate true savings.
