After-School Homework Coach

Help kids with homework in small groups. Perfect for stay-at-home parents or anyone who's good with kids!

üéØ What You'll Achieve

You'll run after-school homework help sessions for 2-4 kids at a time. Parents drop off their kids after school, you help them finish homework, and parents pick them up stress-free. It's like a mini study hall in your home or at a library!

üéí What You Need to Start

Good news: You probably already have most of this!

  • A quiet space with a table
    This can be your dining room, a spare room, or even a library meeting room. Kids need a place to spread out their books and work.

    Good spaces to use:

    • Your dining room table - Most common choice
    • Library meeting room - Free to reserve in most towns
    • Church fellowship hall - Often available for free
    • Community center - May have small rental fee
  • Basic school supplies
    Pencils, erasers, paper, a calculator, and some colored pencils. That's it! Kids usually bring their own stuff too.
    üíµ About $20 Easy
  • Healthy snacks (optional but recommended)
    Kids learn better when they're not hungry! Simple stuff like apple slices, crackers, or pretzels works great. You can include this in your price or ask parents to send snacks.
  • A simple sign-in/sign-out sheet
    This keeps track of when each kid arrives and leaves. Parents love seeing this - it shows you're organized and responsible.

üìã Step-by-Step: Getting Started

Follow these steps in order. Don't skip ahead - each step builds on the last!

  • Step 1: Decide on your schedule
    Most homework coaches work 3-5pm on school days. Pick the days that work for you. Starting with just 2-3 days per week is smart!

    Questions to ask yourself:

    • What time do local schools let out? (Usually 2:30-3:30pm)
    • Which days work best for YOUR schedule?
    • Do you want to do this every week or just some weeks?
    ‚è±Ô∏è 15 minutes Easy
  • Step 2: Set your price
    $15-20 per child per session is typical for beginners. As you get more experience and reviews, you can charge $25-30. Remember: parents are paying for peace of mind!

    Pricing examples:

    • $15/session - Good starting price, easy to fill spots
    • $20/session - Sweet spot for most areas
    • $25-30/session - After you have good reviews
    • Weekly discount: "Pay for 4 sessions, get 5th free!"
    ‚è±Ô∏è 10 minutes Easy
  • Step 3: Create a simple flyer
    You don't need anything fancy! Use Canva (free) or even just type it up in Word. Include: what you offer, days/times, price, and your phone number.

    What to put on your flyer:

    • ‚úÖ A catchy headline: "Homework Help for Busy Parents!"
    • ‚úÖ What you offer: "After-school homework support in a small group"
    • ‚úÖ Days and times: "Mon/Wed/Fri, 3-5pm"
    • ‚úÖ Price: "$20 per session"
    • ‚úÖ Your name and phone number
    • ‚úÖ A friendly photo of you (optional but helps!)
    ‚è±Ô∏è 30 minutes Easy
  • Step 4: Start with people you know
    Your first clients should be friends, neighbors, or family members with kids. This gives you practice and gets you your first reviews!

    People to ask first:

    • Neighbors with school-age kids
    • Friends from church or community groups
    • Parents you know from your own kids' school
    • Family members (cousins, siblings with kids)
    ‚è±Ô∏è 1 hour Easy
  • Step 5: Run your first session
    Keep it simple! Have kids take out their homework, help them one at a time, and give them a small snack break. 2 hours goes by fast!

    Sample 2-hour schedule:

    • 3:00-3:15 - Kids arrive, settle in, get out homework
    • 3:15-4:00 - Homework time (you rotate helping each kid)
    • 4:00-4:15 - Snack break!
    • 4:15-4:50 - Finish homework or quiet reading
    • 4:50-5:00 - Pack up, parents arrive
    ‚è±Ô∏è 2 hours Medium
  • Step 6: Ask for reviews and referrals
    After a few sessions, ask happy parents to tell their friends or write you a review. Word of mouth is the #1 way homework coaches grow!
    ‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes Easy

üìÑ Hang Flyers at These Places

Physical flyers still work great for local services! Most of these places have bulletin boards.

Best places to hang flyers:

  • Library - Parents are there with kids all the time
  • Grocery store bulletin board - High traffic
  • YMCA or community center - Family-focused
  • Pediatrician's office - Ask if you can leave flyers
  • Dance studios/karate schools - Parents wait there
  • Churches - Often have community boards
  • Laundromats - People have time to read
