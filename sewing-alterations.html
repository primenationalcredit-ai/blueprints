üìã Your Sewing Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Master key alteration skills
Practice on your own clothes first! Master hemming, taking in/letting out seams, zipper replacement, working with different fabrics. Precision and consistency are key!
‚úì
Step 2: Set up your workspace
Quality sewing machine, iron and board, good lighting, organized supplies. Create a dedicated space - even a corner works. Clients may come for fittings so keep it tidy!
‚úì
Step 3: Research local competition and set prices
Call local alteration shops for price comparisons. Price by complexity, not just time! Create a clear price list. Start competitive, raise as demand grows.
‚úì
Step 4: Set up online presence
Google Business Profile (essential!), Nextdoor, Facebook. Yelp listing. Take before/after photos of your work. Reviews are everything!
‚úì
Step 5: Build referral partnerships
Visit local dry cleaners - offer to take their overflow. Connect with bridal shops. Leave business cards. These partnerships bring steady work!
‚úì
Step 6: Book your first clients
Start with friends and family at a discount. Post on Nextdoor. Every job well done = referrals. Under-promise, over-deliver!
‚úì
Step 7: Deliver quality work on time
Consistency builds reputation. Meet deadlines - don't over-commit! Quality work brings repeat customers and referrals. Ask for reviews!
‚úì
Step 8: Expand to higher-ticket services
Bridal alterations ($150-500+), custom garments ($100-500+), costume making, sewing classes. As skills grow, so do rates!
