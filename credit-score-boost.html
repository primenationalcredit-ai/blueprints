üìã How to Dispute Errors on Your Credit Report

34% of people have errors on their credit reports!

  • 1
    Get your free reports from all 3 bureaus
    Go to AnnualCreditReport.com (the only official free source)
  • 2
    Review each report carefully
    Look for: wrong accounts, incorrect balances, late payments that weren't late, accounts that aren't yours
  • 3
    File disputes directly with each bureau
    Online: Equifax.com, Experian.com, TransUnion.com (or by mail for paper trail)
  • 4
    Include documentation
    Attach proof of your claim (statements, payment confirmations, etc.)
  • 5
    Wait 30-45 days for investigation
    Bureaus must investigate and respond within this timeframe

üìû Goodwill Letter Template (for removing late payments)

"Dear [Creditor Name],

I am writing to request a goodwill adjustment on my account [Account Number]. I have been a loyal customer since [Year] and have consistently made payments on time, except for [Date of Late Payment].

This late payment was due to [brief honest explanation - job loss, medical issue, etc.]. I have since corrected the situation and have not missed any payments since.

I am asking that you consider removing this late payment from my credit report as a gesture of goodwill. I value my relationship with [Company] and hope to continue as a customer.

Thank you for your consideration.
Sincerely, [Your Name]"

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Credit Score Myths

‚ùå MYTH: Checking your own credit hurts your score

TRUTH: Checking your own credit is a "soft inquiry" and has NO impact on your score. Check often!

‚ùå MYTH: You need to carry a balance to build credit

TRUTH: You do NOT need to pay interest to build credit. Pay your balance in full every month!

‚ùå MYTH: Closing old cards will help your score

TRUTH: Closing cards hurts your score by increasing utilization and shortening credit history.

‚ùå MYTH: You only have one credit score

TRUTH: You have dozens of scores! FICO, VantageScore, and each bureau calculates differently.
