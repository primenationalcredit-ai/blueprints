üéØ The Priority Order

üìã Where Your Side Income Should Go (In Order)

  1. Mini Emergency Fund ($500-$1,000) - Before anything else, have enough to cover a flat tire or small emergency
  2. High-Interest Debt (20%+ APR) - Credit cards are emergencies. Every extra dollar here saves you money
  3. 401(k) Match - If your employer matches, get the FREE money (that's 100% return!)
  4. Full Emergency Fund (3-6 months) - Job loss protection. Sleep better at night
  5. Medium-Interest Debt (8-20%) - Car loans, personal loans, etc.
  6. Retirement (Roth IRA / more 401k) - Future you will thank present you
  7. Other Goals - House down payment, vacation, fun money

üí° How to Apply This

üî• Scenario 1: Credit Card Debt + No Emergency Fund

Side income: $500/month

  • Month 1-2: Build $1,000 mini emergency fund
  • Month 3+: ALL extra to credit card debt (highest APR first)
  • Once CC debt is gone: Build full emergency fund

üí≥ Scenario 2: Credit Card Debt + Some Emergency Fund

Side income: $500/month

  • If you have $1,000+ saved: Attack debt aggressively
  • 100% of side income ‚Üí highest APR credit card
  • Consider balance transfer to 0% card while paying down

‚ú® Scenario 3: No Debt, Building Wealth

Side income: $500/month

  • Max out 401(k) match first (free money!)
  • Build 3-6 month emergency fund in HYSA (4-5% APY)
  • Open Roth IRA and contribute ($7,000/year limit)
  • Remainder to taxable brokerage or specific goals

üè† Scenario 4: Saving for House Down Payment

Side income: $500/month

  • First: Ensure emergency fund is solid
  • If buying in 1-2 years: High-yield savings account
  • If buying in 5+ years: Consider I-bonds or conservative investments
  • Don't neglect retirement - split 70/30 (house/retirement)

üí° Pro Tips

üéØ Maximize Impact

  • Automate it - Transfer side income to its destination immediately
  • Don't lifestyle creep - Treat side income as if it doesn't exist for spending
  • Track your progress - Watch debt go down or savings go up
  • Celebrate milestones - Small reward when you hit goals
  • Keep separate accounts - Side income ‚Üí savings account, not checking

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes

  • Ignoring 401(k) match - That's literally free money you're leaving behind
  • Paying low-interest debt before high - Math says attack highest APR first
  • No emergency fund - One car repair puts you back into debt
  • Investing while in CC debt - No investment beats 22% guaranteed return
