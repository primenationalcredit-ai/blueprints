üìã Your Calligraphy Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Learn calligraphy fundamentals
Master one style first - pointed pen (Copperplate or modern calligraphy) is most popular for weddings. Practice basic strokes before full letters. Takes 3-6 months of daily practice to be client-ready!
‚úì
Step 2: Get quality supplies
Oblique pen holder, pointed nibs (Nikko G to start), quality inks, practice paper, light box for guidelines. Start with ~$100-150 in supplies. Quality tools make a difference!
‚úì
Step 3: Practice daily and develop your style
30+ minutes daily! Work on consistency first, then develop your signature style. Unique style sets you apart. Create alphabet samples, flourish options, addressing styles.
‚úì
Step 4: Build your portfolio
Create sample envelopes, place cards, signs. Photograph everything beautifully! Use mockups. Do free work for friends' weddings to build real samples and testimonials.
‚úì
Step 5: Set up your online presence
Instagram is essential! Post work regularly, use wedding hashtags (#calligraphy #weddingcalligraphy). List on The Knot, WeddingWire. Consider Etsy shop for digital products.
‚úì
Step 6: Connect with wedding vendors
Reach out to wedding planners, stationery designers, photographers, venues. Offer collaboration. Become their recommended calligrapher! Referrals are gold!
‚úì
Step 7: Deliver beautiful work on time
Quality AND deadlines matter for weddings! Build buffer time into schedules. Double-check spellings. Package beautifully. Happy couples leave reviews and refer friends!
‚úì
Step 8: Expand your offerings
Add workshops ($50-150/person), digital fonts and templates (passive income!), live event calligraphy ($200-500/hr), corporate gift personalization, brand lettering!
