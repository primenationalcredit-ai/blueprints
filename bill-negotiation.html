Why This Works

Companies spend 5-10X more to acquire new customers than to keep existing ones. They have retention departments with authority to give discounts. You just have to ASK.

Before You Call

  • Have your current bill amount ready
  • Know how long you've been a customer
  • Research competitor prices (takes 5 min on Google)
  • Be prepared to wait on hold (use speakerphone)
  • Have a notepad ready to write down names and offers

Phone/Internet Bill Script

Step 1: Ask for Retention

"Hi, I'd like to speak with someone about lowering my bill or I'll need to cancel my service."

Step 2: State Your Case

"I've been a customer for [X years] and I'm looking at my options. I see [Competitor] is offering [price] for similar service. I'd like to stay, but I need a better rate."

Step 3: If They Say No

"I understand. Can you transfer me to your retention or loyalty department? I'd like to discuss cancellation options."

Step 4: Get the Deal

"That's helpful. Can you also waive any fees and lock this rate for 12 months? I want to make sure it doesn't jump back up."

Insurance Bill Script

Step 1: Open the Conversation

"Hi, I'm reviewing my insurance costs and I'd like to see what discounts I might be missing."

Step 2: Ask About Discounts

"Can you check if I qualify for: multi-policy discount, safe driver discount, low mileage discount, autopay discount, or loyalty discount?"

Step 3: Mention Competitors

"I got a quote from [Competitor] for $[amount]. I'd prefer to stay with you, but I need my rate closer to that."

Step 4: Increase Deductible

"What would my premium be if I increased my deductible to $1,000?"

Your Negotiation Checklist

  • Phone bill: Call and ask for retention department
  • Internet bill: Mention competitor prices
  • Car insurance: Ask about all available discounts
  • Home/renters insurance: Bundle with auto for savings
  • Cable/streaming: Threaten to cancel (they'll offer deals)
  • Credit card APR: Ask for rate reduction
  • Gym membership: Ask for pause or reduced rate

After Each Call

  • Write down the rep's name and confirmation number
  • Note the new price and when it takes effect
  • Set a calendar reminder to renegotiate in 12 months
  • Move the savings to your Emergency Fund!
