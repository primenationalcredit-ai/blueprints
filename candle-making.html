üí° The 3x Rule for Pricing

Price your candles at 3-4x your cost. If an 8oz candle costs $5 to make (wax, fragrance, wick, jar, label), price it at $20-25 retail. This covers materials, your time, Etsy fees, shipping supplies, and still leaves healthy profit!

üìã Your Candle Business Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Learn Candle Making Fundamentals
Study wax types (soy, coconut, parasoy blends), proper wick sizing, fragrance load percentages (8-10%), and pour temperatures. Watch YouTube tutorials from CandleScience and Standley Handcrafted. Understanding the science prevents costly mistakes!
‚úì
Step 2: Get Your Starter Supplies
Order a starter kit or buy: 10 lbs soy wax ($25-40), 3-4 fragrance oils ($30-50), wicks in multiple sizes ($15), containers ($20-40), thermometer, pour pot, and scale. Budget $100-200 to start. CandleScience kits are perfect for beginners!
‚úì
Step 3: Test, Test, Test!
Make test batches before selling. Test different wicks in your containers (burn test for 4+ hours). Test fragrance oils for hot throw (scent when burning) and cold throw (scent when unlit). Document everything! Proper testing takes 2-4 weeks but prevents customer complaints.
‚úì
Step 4: Develop Your Brand & Signature Scents
Create a memorable brand name and visual identity. Develop 5-8 signature scents that tell a story. Design professional labels (Canva works great). Your brand is what justifies premium pricing‚Äîgeneric = commodity, branded = boutique!
‚úì
Step 5: Take Amazing Product Photos
Photos sell candles! Use natural lighting, clean backgrounds, and lifestyle shots showing candles in beautiful settings. Show the flame, the label, the packaging. Take photos from multiple angles. One great photo shoot can fuel months of marketing!
‚úì
Step 6: Open Your Etsy Shop
Create your Etsy shop with optimized listings. Write detailed descriptions with keywords. Set competitive pricing (research similar candles). Offer multiple sizes and bundle options. Enable Etsy Ads once you have reviews. Your first 10 sales/reviews are crucial for algorithm ranking!
‚úì
Step 7: Expand Sales Channels
Once Etsy is running, add channels: apply to local craft markets, approach boutiques for wholesale, set up Instagram shopping, consider your own Shopify site. Multiple channels = stable income. Don't put all eggs in one basket!
‚úì
Step 8: Scale with Efficiency and Wholesale
Buy supplies in bulk for better margins. Create production batches (pour 20-50 candles at once). Develop seasonal collections. Land wholesale accounts with local boutiques. Consider subscription boxes or private labeling. Your side hustle can become a full business!

üí° Pro Tips for Success

üí° Seasonal Scents = Seasonal Sales

Plan collections 2-3 months ahead. Pumpkin spice in August, Christmas scents in October, fresh florals in February. Holiday seasons (Oct-Dec) can account for 40-50% of annual sales. Don't miss the window!

üí° The Power of Samples

Include a wax melt sample with every order. Costs you $0.50-1, but introduces customers to new scents. If they love it, they'll order the full candle. Samples convert browsers into repeat buyers!

‚ö†Ô∏è Safety & Legal Requirements

Candles are regulated products. Include proper warning labels on every candle (burn instructions, fire safety). Check your state's requirements for selling home goods. Consider product liability insurance ($200-500/year). Safety first protects you AND your customers!
