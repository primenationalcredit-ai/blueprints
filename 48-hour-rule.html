üéØ What Is The 48-Hour Rule?

Simple: Before buying ANYTHING over $50, you must wait 48 hours.

That's it. See something you want? Write it down. Wait 48 hours. If you still want it AND can afford it, buy it. Most of the time? You'll forget about it or realize you didn't actually need it.

üß† The Science Behind It

Impulse purchases trigger dopamine - the "feel good" chemical. That rush fades within 24-48 hours. By waiting, you let the emotional high pass and make decisions with your logical brain instead!

üìã How to Use The 48-Hour Rule

1 Set Your Threshold

Decide your dollar amount. We recommend $50, but you can adjust:

  • $25 - If you're really trying to cut spending
  • $50 - The sweet spot for most people
  • $100 - If you have more financial flexibility
  • I've set my threshold at: $______

2 Create Your "Want List"

When you want to buy something over your threshold, DON'T BUY IT. Instead:

  • Create a note on your phone called "48-Hour Wait List"
  • Write down: Item name, price, date/time you wanted it
  • Set a reminder for exactly 48 hours later
üìù Example Entry:
"Nike Air Max - $129 - Dec 23, 3:00pm - Check again Dec 25, 3:00pm"

3 Ask These 5 Questions (After 48 Hours)

When your reminder goes off, ask yourself:

1. Do I still want this?

Often the answer is "I forgot about it" or "Not really"

2. Can I actually afford it?

Not "can I put it on a card" - do I have cash for this?

3. Do I already own something similar?

Check your closet/garage/drawers first

4. What else could this money do?

Emergency fund? Debt payoff? Something you need more?

5. Will this matter in 30 days?

If not, it's probably not worth buying

4 Make Your Decision

After 48 hours and asking the 5 questions:

  • If YES to all 5: Buy it guilt-free! You made a thoughtful decision.
  • If NO to any: Delete it from your list. You just saved money!
  • I understand the decision framework

5 Track Your Wins

Keep a running total of money you DIDN'T spend. This is motivating!

  • Start a "Money Saved" note on my phone
  • Add each item I decided NOT to buy
  • Watch my savings grow!

‚ö†Ô∏è Exceptions to the Rule

The 48-hour rule does NOT apply to:

  • ‚úÖ True emergencies (car breakdown, medical needs)
  • ‚úÖ Planned purchases you've already budgeted for
  • ‚úÖ Groceries and essential household items
  • ‚úÖ Bills and recurring expenses
üö´ Watch Out For Fake Urgency!
"Sale ends tonight!" and "Only 2 left!" are marketing tricks. Real deals come back. If you miss a sale, another one will happen. Don't let fake urgency override your 48-hour rule!
