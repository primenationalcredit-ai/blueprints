1 Calculate Your Current Debt & Interest

Before applying for a balance transfer card, know exactly what you're working with:

  • List all your credit cards with balances
  • Note the APR (interest rate) on each card
  • Add up your total high-interest debt
  • Calculate monthly interest you're currently paying
üí° Quick Interest Calculator: Take your balance √ó your APR √∑ 12 = monthly interest. Example: $5,000 √ó 0.24 √∑ 12 = $100/month in interest alone!

2 Check Your Credit Score

Balance transfer cards typically require good to excellent credit:

  • 670-739 (Good): Qualify for decent offers (12-15 months 0%)
  • 740-799 (Very Good): Qualify for great offers (15-18 months 0%)
  • 800+ (Excellent): Qualify for best offers (18-21 months 0%)
  • Check your score free at Credit Karma, Credit Sesame, or your bank
  • Review your credit report for errors

3 Compare Balance Transfer Cards

Look for cards with the longest 0% period and lowest transfer fees:

Feature What to Look For
0% APR Period 15-21 months (longer is better!)
Balance Transfer Fee 3-5% of amount transferred (some cards have $0!)
Regular APR After Check what rate kicks in after promo ends
Credit Limit High enough to transfer your full balance
  • Research top balance transfer cards online
  • Compare at least 3 different offers
  • Calculate total cost (transfer fee) for each

4 Apply for the Best Card

Once you've chosen your card, apply online (takes 10-15 minutes):

  • Have your income info ready
  • Know your monthly housing payment
  • Complete the online application
  • Get instant or wait for approval decision
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Only apply for ONE card! Multiple applications hurt your credit score.

5 Request the Balance Transfer

Once approved, initiate the transfer immediately:

  • Log into your new card account
  • Find "Balance Transfer" option
  • Enter your OLD card info (card number, amount to transfer)
  • Submit the transfer request
  • Continue paying OLD card until transfer completes (7-14 days)
‚úÖ Pro Tip: Do this within the first 60 days to qualify for the promotional 0% rate!

6 Create Your Payoff Plan

The 0% period is your window to crush this debt! Make a plan:

  • DO NOT use the new card for purchases!

7 Avoid These Mistakes!

üö´ Common Balance Transfer Mistakes:
  • Missing a payment: Can cancel your 0% rate instantly!
  • Using the card for purchases: New purchases often don't get 0%
  • Not paying it off in time: Interest kicks in on remaining balance
  • Closing old cards: Hurts your credit utilization ratio
  • Racking up debt on old cards: You'll be worse off than before!
