‚úì
Step 1: Buy your supplies
Pooper scooper, heavy-duty bags, bucket with lid, gloves. Under $50 total. Get hand sanitizer and paper towels for your car too.
‚úì
Step 2: Set your prices
Research local competition. Most charge $12-15/week for one dog. Decide on add-on pricing for extra dogs and services.
‚úì
Step 3: Pick your service area
Start with 2-3 neighborhoods within 10 minutes of home. Target subdivisions with fenced yards, HOAs (people care about appearance), and busy professionals.
‚úì
Step 4: Create a simple flyer
Headline: "Tired of Scooping Poop? Let Me Do It!" List services, price, phone number. Use Canva (free) for a professional look.
‚úì
Step 5: Go door-to-door (fastest method!)
Walk neighborhoods looking for dog signs: toys in yard, "beware of dog" signs, worn paths in grass, barking dogs. Knock on those doors!
‚úì
Step 6: Post on Nextdoor and Facebook groups
Nextdoor is perfect - neighbors helping neighbors! Join local dog owner groups on Facebook. These people love their dogs but hate cleanup!
‚úì
Step 7: Do excellent work on your first customers
Walk the ENTIRE yard in a grid pattern. Check bushes and fence lines. Text when done. Take before/after photos for marketing!
‚úì
Step 8: Set up billing and ask for referrals
Use Venmo, Zelle, or Cash App. Many prefer paying monthly. After 2-3 weeks, ask: "Know any neighbors who might want this?" Offer $5 off for referrals!
