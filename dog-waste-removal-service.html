üí° Why This Business Works

üìã Step-by-Step: Getting Started

Follow these steps and you could have your first paying customer THIS WEEK!

  • Step 1: Set your prices
    Most people charge $10-15 per week for one visit. Offer discounts for twice-weekly service or multiple dogs.

    Sample pricing:

    • 1 dog, once/week: $12-15
    • 1 dog, twice/week: $20-25
    • Each additional dog: +$3-5
    • First-time cleanup (if yard is really bad): $25-50 one-time fee
    ‚è±Ô∏è 10 minutes Easy
  • Step 2: Pick your service area
    Start small! Pick 2-3 neighborhoods within 10 minutes of your home. This keeps your driving time down and profits up.

    Good neighborhoods to target:

    • Neighborhoods with lots of fenced yards
    • Subdivisions with HOAs (people care about yard appearance)
    • Areas with busy professionals (they don't have time)
    • Neighborhoods with older residents (harder for them to do)
    ‚è±Ô∏è 15 minutes Easy
  • Step 3: Create a simple flyer
    Keep it simple! A catchy headline, what you do, your price, and your phone number. Use Canva (free) or just type it in Word.

    What to put on your flyer:

    • Headline: "Tired of Scooping Poop? Let Me Do It!"
    • What you do: "Weekly yard cleanup for dog owners"
    • Price: "Starting at $12/week"
    • Your phone number
    • Optional: A cute dog picture or poop emoji üí©
    ‚è±Ô∏è 30 minutes Easy
  • Step 4: Go door-to-door (the fastest way!)
    Walk through your target neighborhoods. Look for houses with dog toys in the yard, "beware of dog" signs, or dogs barking. Knock on those doors!

    Signs a house has a dog:

    • Dog toys or bowls in the yard
    • Worn paths in the grass
    • Dog barking when you approach
    • "Beware of Dog" or dog breed signs
    • Visible dog door
    ‚è±Ô∏è 2-3 hours Medium
  • Step 5: Do your first cleanup
    Show up on time, do a thorough job, and let the customer know when you're done. Take a "before and after" photo for your own marketing!

    Pro tips for your first job:

    • Walk the ENTIRE yard in a grid pattern so you don't miss spots
    • Check around bushes and along fence lines
    • Leave a "we were here" door hanger or text the customer
    • Take the waste with you - don't leave it in their trash
    ‚è±Ô∏è 15-20 min/yard Easy
  • Step 6: Set up weekly billing
    Use Venmo, Zelle, Cash App, or just collect cash. Many people like to pay monthly in advance. Make it easy for them!
    ‚è±Ô∏è 15 minutes Easy
  • Step 7: Ask for referrals
    After 2-3 weeks of service, ask happy customers: "Do you know any neighbors with dogs who might want this service?" Offer $5 off their next month for every referral.
    ‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes Easy
