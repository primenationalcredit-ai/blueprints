üìã Your Energy Savings Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Do a 10-minute walk-through
Walk through your home and note: lights left on, devices on standby, drafty windows/doors, thermostat settings. Make a list of quick fixes.
‚úì
Step 2: Optimize your thermostat
Set to 68¬∞F in winter, 78¬∞F in summer. If you have a programmable thermostat, set it to reduce 8-10¬∞ when you're asleep or away.
‚úì
Step 3: Lower your water heater
Find your water heater and turn it down to 120¬∞F (look for a dial on the front or bottom). If you can't find it, check the manual or call a plumber.
‚úì
Step 4: Tackle energy vampires
Get power strips for TV area, computer desk, and kitchen appliances. Flip them off when not in use. Or just unplug chargers and small appliances.
‚úì
Step 5: Replace your most-used bulbs with LEDs
Identify your 5 most-used light fixtures and replace those bulbs first. LEDs are $2-5 each at any hardware store.
‚úì
Step 6: Check and replace your HVAC filter
Find your air return vent (usually in hallway or near furnace). Remove the filter - if you can't see through it, replace it. Set a monthly reminder!
‚úì
Step 7: Seal obvious air leaks
Feel for drafts around doors and windows. Apply weatherstripping or caulk to gaps. Don't forget outlet covers on exterior walls!
‚úì
Step 8: Change your laundry habits
Switch to cold water washing, only run full loads, and clean the lint trap before every dryer load. Consider line drying when possible.

‚ùå Common Energy Myths

"Turning lights on/off uses more energy than leaving them on"
‚úÖ FALSE - Always turn off lights when leaving a room. The "surge" myth is busted!
"Cranking the thermostat heats/cools faster"
‚úÖ FALSE - Your HVAC works at one speed. Setting it to 90¬∞ doesn't heat faster than 72¬∞.
"Closing vents in unused rooms saves energy"
‚úÖ FALSE - Can actually damage your HVAC system and increase costs.
"Leaving ceiling fans on cools the room"
‚úÖ FALSE - Fans cool people, not rooms. Turn them off when you leave.
"Screen savers save energy"
‚úÖ FALSE - Screen savers use MORE energy than sleep mode. Set your computer to sleep.
