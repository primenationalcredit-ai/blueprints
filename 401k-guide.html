ü§î Wait, What's a 401(k)?

Let Me Explain Like You're 10 Years Old:

A 401(k) is a special savings account for when you're old and stop working (retirement). The money comes out of your paycheck BEFORE taxes, which means you pay less in taxes!

Think of it like this:

‚Ä¢ Regular savings = You earn $100, pay $25 in taxes, save $75

‚Ä¢ 401(k) savings = You save $100 FIRST, then pay taxes on the remaining $75

You end up saving MORE money and paying LESS taxes. Win-win!

üéØ Step 5: Pick Your Investments (Easy Mode)

Good news: You don't need to be a stock market expert! There's ONE easy choice that works great for most people:

üéØ Pick a "Target Date Fund"!

What's a Target Date Fund?

It's a fund that automatically invests your money based on when you plan to retire. You pick the year, and it does ALL the work for you!

Example names:

‚Ä¢ "Target 2055 Fund" (if you'll retire around 2055)

‚Ä¢ "Retirement 2050 Fund" (if you'll retire around 2050)

‚Ä¢ "LifePath 2060 Fund" (if you'll retire around 2060)

How to Pick Your Year:

1 Think about when you want to retire (usually age 65)

2 Subtract your current age from 65

3 Add that number to today's year

4 Pick the fund closest to that year!

üìù Example Calculation:

You're 30 years old. It's 2025. You want to retire at 65.

65 - 30 = 35 years until retirement

2025 + 35 = 2060

üëâ Pick: "Target 2060 Fund" (or 2055 or 2065 if 2060 isn't available)

üéâ Why Target Date Funds Are Awesome:

‚úÖ Automatically diversified (mix of stocks and bonds)

‚úÖ Gets safer as you get closer to retirement

‚úÖ Set it and forget it‚Äîno maintenance needed!

‚úÖ Perfect for beginners (and experts too!)

‚è∞ Important: What Is Vesting?

Quick Explanation:

"Vesting" means how long you need to work at your company before you get to KEEP the matching money if you leave.

Common Vesting Schedules:

üü¢ Immediate (Best!): You own 100% of the match right away

üü° Cliff (3-year): You own 0% until year 3, then 100%

üü† Graded: 20% after year 1, 40% after year 2, 60% year 3, 80% year 4, 100% year 5

üí° Here's What Matters:

STILL contribute to get the match! Even if you're not fully vested, you might be by the time you leave. And YOUR contributions are always 100% yours‚Äîonly the employer match has vesting.
