üìã Your Stock Photography Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Research What Actually Sells
Before shooting, browse Shutterstock and Adobe Stock for trending searches and popular categories. Look at top-selling images in your areas of interest. Note what makes them work: lighting, composition, subjects. Don't guess‚Äîstudy the market first!
‚úì
Step 2: Start Shooting with Purpose
Use your smartphone or camera to capture commercial-quality images. Focus on good lighting (natural light is best), sharp focus, and clean compositions. Think like a buyer: "Would a business use this for their website or marketing?" Shoot in high resolution!
‚úì
Step 3: Sign Up on Multiple Platforms
Create contributor accounts on Shutterstock, Adobe Stock, and at least one other platform. Each requires a small initial portfolio review (5-10 images). Submit your best work to get approved. It's free to join all of them!
‚úì
Step 4: Master Keywording (This Is Critical!)
Keywords determine if buyers find your photos. Use 20-50 relevant, specific keywords per image. Include: the subject, colors, emotions, concepts, location, and use cases. "Woman working from home laptop coffee morning remote" beats just "woman." Keywords = discoverability = sales!
‚úì
Step 5: Upload Your First 50 Photos
Batch upload your best work. Aim for 50 solid images to start across multiple categories. Quality matters more than quantity early on‚Äîrejected photos hurt your acceptance rate. Review each platform's technical requirements (resolution, format)!
‚úì
Step 6: Create a Consistent Upload Schedule
Commit to uploading 10-20 new photos per week. Set aside time on weekends for shooting and weekday evenings for editing/uploading. Consistency beats bursts‚Äîtreat it like a side business. In 6 months, you'll have 300+ images earning for you!
‚úì
Step 7: Analyze What's Working
After 2-3 months, check your stats. Which photos sell most? Which categories perform best? Double down on what works. If business photos sell well, shoot more business content. Let the data guide your shooting strategy!
‚úì
Step 8: Scale with People & Model Releases
Photos with people sell 3-5x more than objects/landscapes. Recruit friends and family as models (get signed model releases!). Diverse, authentic people shots are in huge demand. This is how top contributors hit $1,000+/month!

üí° Pro Tips for More Sales

üí° Think "Copy Space"

Leave room for text! Designers love images with empty space where they can add headlines, logos, or text. A beautiful landscape with blank sky = usable for marketing. Edge-to-edge compositions are hard to use commercially!

üí° Shoot Horizontal AND Vertical

Capture both orientations of every scene. Websites need horizontal, Instagram needs vertical, both need square crops. Triple your earning potential from every shoot by delivering multiple orientations!

üí° Chase Trends Early

Be first to new topics. When AI took off, early AI-themed photos sold like crazy. Watch business news, social trends, and emerging topics. Seasonal content (holidays, back-to-school) should be uploaded 2-3 months BEFORE the season!

‚ö†Ô∏è Model & Property Releases Required

No releases = no commercial sales. Any recognizable person needs a signed model release. Private property, logos, and branded items need property releases. Without releases, your photos can only sell for "editorial" use (much smaller market). Get releases for everything!
