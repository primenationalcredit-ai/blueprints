üìã Your Benefits Application Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Use Benefits.gov screener
Visit Benefits.gov and answer questions about your situation. It will identify ALL federal and state programs you may qualify for based on your specific circumstances.
‚úì
Step 2: Apply for SNAP (food assistance)
Apply online at your state's SNAP website, in person at your local office, or by phone. Average benefit is $234/month per person. You can use benefits immediately once approved!
‚úì
Step 3: Apply for Medicaid (healthcare)
Apply through Healthcare.gov or your state Medicaid office. Covers doctor visits, hospital, prescriptions, mental health, and more. Apply any time - no enrollment period!
‚úì
Step 4: Apply for LIHEAP (energy assistance)
Contact your local Community Action Agency. LIHEAP helps pay heating/cooling bills - can provide $200-1,000+ per year. Apply before winter for heating assistance!
‚úì
Step 5: Check for WIC (families with young children)
If pregnant, breastfeeding, or have children under 5, apply for WIC. Provides free healthy foods, nutrition education, and breastfeeding support.
‚úì
Step 6: Apply for Lifeline phone discount
$9.25/month off phone service. Apply at lifelinesupport.org. If you receive SNAP, Medicaid, or SSI, you automatically qualify!
‚úì
Step 7: Apply for housing assistance
Section 8 vouchers have long waitlists (2-5+ years), so apply NOW even if you don't need it immediately. Contact your local Housing Authority.
‚úì
Step 8: Call 211 for local resources
Dial 2-1-1 for local assistance with food, housing, utilities, and more. They know about programs specific to your area that you might miss!
