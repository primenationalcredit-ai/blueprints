üìã Major Programs You May Qualify For

üçé SNAP (Food Stamps)

Help buying groceries

What It Provides:

Monthly funds loaded onto an EBT card to buy groceries. Average benefit is $234/month per person.

Who Qualifies:

  • Income at or below 130% of poverty line (see table above)
  • Assets under $2,750 (or $4,500 if 60+ or disabled)
  • Must be US citizen or qualified non-citizen
  • Must meet work requirements if able-bodied adult without dependents

How to Apply:

Apply online at your state's SNAP website, in person at your local SNAP office, or by mail.

Find Your State SNAP Office ‚Üí

üè• Medicaid

Free or low-cost health insurance

What It Provides:

Comprehensive health coverage including doctor visits, hospital stays, prescriptions, mental health, and more.

Who Qualifies:

  • Income up to 138% of poverty level in most states
  • Pregnant women, children, elderly, and disabled have higher limits
  • Parents and caretakers of minor children
  • No asset test in most states

How to Apply:

Apply through Healthcare.gov during open enrollment, or directly through your state Medicaid office anytime.

Check Medicaid Eligibility ‚Üí

üí° LIHEAP (Utility Assistance)

Help paying heating & cooling bills

What It Provides:

Helps pay home energy bills. Can provide one-time payment or ongoing assistance. Also helps with weatherization.

Who Qualifies:

  • Income up to 150% of poverty level (higher in some states)
  • Priority given to elderly, disabled, and families with young children
  • Must be responsible for home energy costs

How to Apply:

Contact your local Community Action Agency or state LIHEAP office. Apply before winter for heating assistance!

Find LIHEAP in Your State ‚Üí

üçº WIC (Women, Infants & Children)

Nutrition for pregnant women & young children

What It Provides:

Free healthy foods, nutrition education, and breastfeeding support for pregnant/postpartum women and children under 5.

Who Qualifies:

  • Pregnant, breastfeeding, or postpartum women
  • Infants and children under age 5
  • Income up to 185% of poverty level
  • Must have a "nutritional risk" (determined at appointment)

How to Apply:

Contact your local WIC office for an appointment. You'll need ID, proof of income, and proof of address.

Find WIC Near You ‚Üí

üè† Section 8 / Housing Choice Voucher

Help paying rent

What It Provides:

Vouchers that pay a portion of your rent directly to your landlord. You typically pay 30% of income toward rent.

Who Qualifies:

  • Income below 50% of area median income
  • US citizen or eligible non-citizen
  • Priority often given to homeless, displaced, or paying >50% of income on rent

Important Note:

Waitlists are often 2-5+ years long. Apply NOW even if you don't need it immediately!

Learn About Section 8 ‚Üí

üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß TANF (Cash Assistance)

Temporary cash help for families

What It Provides:

Monthly cash benefits to help families with children meet basic needs while working toward self-sufficiency.

Who Qualifies:

  • Families with children under 18 (or 19 if in school)
  • Very low income and limited assets
  • Must participate in work activities
  • Lifetime limit of 60 months in most states

How to Apply:

Apply through your state's human services department. Often same application as SNAP.

‚úÖ How to Apply - Step by Step

‚úì
Step 1: Gather your documents
You'll need: Photo ID, Social Security cards for household members, proof of income (pay stubs, tax return), proof of address (utility bill, lease), and bank statements.
‚úì
Step 2: Use a benefits screener to see what you qualify for
Use Benefits.gov or your state's screening tool to check ALL programs at once. Don't assume you won't qualify!
‚úì
Step 3: Apply for SNAP first (fastest to get)
SNAP is often approved within 30 days (7 days for emergencies). Apply online at your state's portal.
‚úì
Step 4: Apply for Medicaid (can be retroactive!)
Medicaid can cover medical bills from up to 3 months BEFORE you apply. Apply even if you have recent medical debt.
‚úì
Step 5: Apply for any other programs you may qualify for
LIHEAP before winter, WIC if pregnant/have young children, Section 8 (long waitlist - apply now!).
‚úì
Step 6: Complete any required interviews
Many programs require a phone or in-person interview. Answer the call! Missing it delays your benefits.
‚úì
Step 7: Report changes and recertify on time
Benefits must be renewed (usually every 6-12 months). Mark your calendar! Report income changes promptly.
