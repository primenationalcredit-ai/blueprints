üìã Your Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Choose your sport
Pick a sport you know well. Soccer is easiest to start. Football pays the most. Basketball has year-round demand. Start with ONE sport!
‚úì
Step 2: Complete certification
Take the NFHS online course for your sport. Then complete your state's requirements (usually a clinic + test). Cost: $50-150 typically.
‚úì
Step 3: Get your equipment
Buy the official uniform for your sport, whistle, flags/cards. Check with your local association‚Äîsometimes they provide equipment to new officials!
‚úì
Step 4: Join your local officials association
Find your local officials chapter. They assign games, provide mentoring, and help you improve. This is essential for getting work!
‚úì
Step 5: Start with youth/rec games
Take lower-level games first to build experience. Youth rec leagues are forgiving. Get comfortable before moving to competitive levels!
‚úì
Step 6: Study the rules constantly
Know the rulebook inside and out. Watch games at higher levels. Get feedback from experienced officials. Never stop learning!
‚úì
Step 7: Move up to higher levels
As you improve, take middle school, then JV, then varsity games. Each level pays more! Aim for varsity within 2-3 years.
‚úì
Step 8: Add playoffs and tournaments
Playoff and tournament games pay extra! State tournaments, travel tournaments. Build reputation for the best assignments.
