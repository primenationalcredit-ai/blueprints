üìã Your APR Negotiation Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Know your current rate
Check your statement or call the number on your card. Know your exact APR - you'll need to reference it in the call.
‚úì
Step 2: Know your leverage points
How long have you been a customer? What's your payment history? Do you have competing offers? Good history = more leverage.
‚úì
Step 3: Research competitor rates
Look up balance transfer offers and other card rates. "I've received an offer from X for Y% APR" is powerful leverage.
‚úì
Step 4: Call and ask for retention
Call the number on your card. If the first rep can't help, ask for the "retention department" or a supervisor - they have more authority.
‚úì
Step 5: Use the scripts below
Be polite but firm. State your case clearly. Mention competitor offers. Ask specifically for a lower rate.
‚úì
Step 6: If denied, ask about promotional rates
"Is there any promotional rate you could offer, even temporarily?" A 6-12 month promo rate still saves money!
‚úì
Step 7: Get confirmation
If successful, confirm new rate and effective date. Ask for email confirmation. Note rep's name and date.
‚úì
Step 8: If denied, try again in 60 days
Different reps = different results. Mark calendar to call again. "HUCA" = Hang Up, Call Again!

üí¨ Negotiation Scripts

üìû Step 1: Get to the Right Person

"Hi, I'd like to speak with someone about lowering my interest rate, please."

(They may transfer you to retention or a supervisor - that's good!)

üìû Step 2: Make Your Case

"Hi, I've been a customer for [X years] and I've always paid on time. I'm currently paying [XX%] APR, which is higher than other offers I'm seeing. I'd like to request a lower rate to stay with [Card Company]. What can you do for me?"

üìû Step 3: If They Say No

"I understand. Is there a supervisor I could speak with? Or is there any promotional rate you could offer, even temporarily? I really would prefer to stay with you."

üìû Step 4: The Competitor Card

"I've received offers from [Competitor] for 0% APR on balance transfers. I'd prefer to stay with you, but I need a competitive rate to make that work."

üìû Step 5: Close the Call

If YES: "Thank you! Can you confirm my new rate and when it takes effect? And could you send that in writing?"

If NO: "Thank you for your time. I'll call back in 60 days to ask again."
