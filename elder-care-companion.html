üìã Your Companion Care Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Get CPR/First Aid certified
Essential for working with seniors. Red Cross or AHA courses cost $50-100. Many families require this before hiring.
‚úì
Step 2: Get background check and TB test
Families and platforms require these. Background check through Care.com or your state. TB test at urgent care ($20-50).
‚úì
Step 3: Consider additional training
Dementia/Alzheimer's training is valuable and lets you charge more. Online courses available for $50-200.
‚úì
Step 4: Get insured and bonded
General liability insurance ($300-600/year) and bonding ($100-300/year) give families peace of mind and protect you.
‚úì
Step 5: Create profiles on care platforms
Care.com and CareLinx connect you with families. Great photos, warm bio, list all certifications. Highlight patience and compassion!
‚úì
Step 6: Network with referral sources
Senior centers, churches, hospital discharge planners, and geriatric care managers all refer families. Introduce yourself!
‚úì
Step 7: Build genuine relationships
Be patient, kind, and really LISTEN. Seniors often just need someone who cares. Genuine connection leads to long-term clients and referrals.
‚úì
Step 8: Build recurring weekly clients
Weekly visits create stable income. 3-4 regular clients at 10 hrs/week each = $720-1,400/week. Reliability builds your reputation!
