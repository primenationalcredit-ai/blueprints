âœ“
Step 1: Analyze your current spending
Look at the last 3 months of grocery spending on your bank statement. Add it up and divide by 3. That's your current monthly average. Your goal is to spend 15-20% less!
âœ“
Step 2: Set your weekly budget
Divide your monthly target by 4. This is your weekly cash amount. Start slightly higher than you think you need - you can always tighten next month.
âœ“
Step 3: Withdraw cash weekly
Every Sunday, withdraw your exact budget in cash from the ATM. Put it in an envelope labeled "GROCERIES." This is ALL you have for the week!
âœ“
Step 4: Leave cards at home!
This is CRITICAL. When you grocery shop, ONLY bring your cash envelope. No debit card, no credit card. No backup = no cheating.
âœ“
Step 5: Track as you shop
Use your phone calculator to keep a running total as you add items to your cart. If you go over budget, put something back!
âœ“
Step 6: Roll over or save leftovers
If you have cash left at the end of the week, you can: (A) Roll it into next week's envelope, or (B) Put it straight into savings!

Fresh foods (produce, meat, dairy) are on the edges of the store. Expensive processed stuff is in the middle aisles. Stick to the perimeter!

âš ï¸ Common Mistakes to Avoid

Don't Bring a "Backup" Card

"Just in case" defeats the entire purpose! The power of cash envelopes is that when the cash is gone, spending STOPS. Leave it at home!

If you currently spend $800/month on groceries, don't try to go to $300 overnight. Start 15-20% lower and adjust each month. Sustainable wins!
