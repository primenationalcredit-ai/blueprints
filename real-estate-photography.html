üìã Your Real Estate Photography Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Learn Real Estate Photography Techniques
Master wide-angle composition, HDR bracketing (3-5 exposures blended), window pull technique, and flash bounce. YouTube is your free film school‚Äîwatch Rich Baum, Nathan Cool, and Eli Jones. Practice shooting rooms in your own home until your photos look professional!
‚úì
Step 2: Get Your Equipment Ready
You need a camera (full-frame ideal, but crop sensor works to start), wide-angle lens (16-35mm equivalent), and sturdy tripod. Budget $1,500-3,000 to start. Buy used from MPB or KEH to save money. Don't forget Lightroom subscription ($10/month) for editing!
‚úì
Step 3: Build Your Portfolio (Shoot for Free)
Shoot 5-10 properties for free to build samples. Ask friends, family, or neighbors if you can photograph their homes. Reach out to agents offering free shoots for their next listing. You need PROOF of your skills before anyone will pay you!
‚úì
Step 4: Create Your Online Presence
Set up an Instagram showcasing your best shots. Create a Google Business Profile. Build a simple portfolio website (Squarespace, Pixieset, or SmugMug). Make it easy for agents to see your work and contact you. Include pricing packages!
‚úì
Step 5: Land Your First Paying Clients
Visit broker offices, email agents directly, attend real estate networking events. Offer competitive intro pricing ($100-150 for first shoot). Focus on being EASY to work with‚Äîfast booking, fast shooting, fast delivery. Reliability beats perfection early on!
‚úì
Step 6: Master Your Workflow (24-Hour Turnaround)
Agents need photos FAST. Create a streamlined workflow: shoot in 30-60 minutes, edit in 1-2 hours, deliver same day or next morning. Use Lightroom presets to speed editing. Fast turnaround = happy agents = repeat business and referrals!
‚úì
Step 7: Build Your Agent Roster
Your goal: 5-10 agents who use you regularly. After each shoot, ask for referrals. Request Google reviews. Stay top-of-mind with occasional check-ins. One busy agent with 3-4 listings/month = $600-1,600/month from just ONE client!
‚úì
Step 8: Add Services and Scale
Once you're busy, add drone photography (get Part 107 certified), video walkthroughs, 3D Matterport tours, and twilight shoots. These add-ons can double your average ticket. Eventually, hire a second shooter to take more jobs without burning out!

üí° Pro Tips for Success

üí° The Staging Conversation

Politely help agents understand staging matters. A cluttered, dark home photographs poorly no matter your skill. Send a "photo prep checklist" before shoots: turn on all lights, open blinds, declutter counters, hide trash cans. Your photos are only as good as the space allows!

üí° Shoot Twilight = Premium Pricing

Twilight/dusk exterior shots command $100-200 extra. That magical blue hour makes homes look luxurious. Offer this as an add-on for higher-end listings. One 20-minute twilight shoot can add 50% to your invoice!

‚ö†Ô∏è Don't Over-Edit

Keep it realistic‚Äîagents get burned by over-processed photos. If buyers show up and the home doesn't match the photos, the agent looks bad. Bright, clean, true-to-life photos build trust. Save the HDR glow for your personal art projects!
