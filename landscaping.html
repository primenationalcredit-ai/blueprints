üìã Your Action Plan

‚úì
Step 1: Learn landscaping fundamentals
Study local plants, growing zones, hardscape techniques, drainage principles, and basic design. Take free online courses, watch YouTube, visit local nurseries for plant knowledge.
‚úì
Step 2: Get basic equipment and transportation
Start with a truck (or rent one), trailer, and basic hand tools. Build relationships with local nurseries and material suppliers for wholesale pricing.
‚úì
Step 3: Get licensed and insured
Check your state for contractor license requirements. Get general liability insurance ($300-500/year). This protects you and makes clients trust you!
‚úì
Step 4: Set up your online presence
Create profiles on Thumbtack, Nextdoor, Google Business, and Houzz. These are FREE (or low cost) and essential for getting found by customers.
‚úì
Step 5: Build your portfolio with before/after photos
Do a few projects at cost for friends/family to get photos. Before/after photos are EVERYTHING in landscaping - they sell your work instantly!
‚úì
Step 6: Learn to estimate accurately
Calculate materials, labor hours, equipment rental, and add 20-30% profit margin. Accurate estimates prevent losses. Use software like Jobber or Aspire to help.
‚úì
Step 7: Deliver quality and collect reviews
Quality work creates referrals. After every project, ask for Google and Nextdoor reviews. Reviews are the lifeblood of a service business!
‚úì
Step 8: Scale with crews and add services
Hire installation crews, add maintenance contracts for recurring revenue, pursue commercial projects, and add irrigation/lighting services as you grow.
