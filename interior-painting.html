üí° The Secret: PREP WORK

80% of a great paint job is preparation. Filling holes, sanding, cleaning walls, taping edges. Rushing prep = visible mistakes. Take your time here - it's what separates amateurs from pros!

Step 1: Learn proper painting techniques
Watch YouTube tutorials (Idaho Painter, The Paint People). Practice on your own home or a friend's room. Master prep work, cutting in, and rolling.
‚úì
Step 2: Invest in quality equipment
Buy quality brushes (Purdy, Wooster) - cheap brushes leave streaks. Get proper ladders, drop cloths, and tape. Quality tools = quality results.
‚úì
Step 3: Paint a few rooms cheap or free
Build portfolio with friends/family. Take professional before/after photos. Get testimonials and practice your process.
‚úì
Step 4: Get licensed and insured
Business license, general liability insurance ($400-800/year). Check if your state requires contractor licensing for painting work.
‚úì
Step 5: Create your pricing guide
Set prices by room type. Always quote AFTER seeing the job in person. Account for prep time, paint cost, and complexity.
‚úì
Step 6: Market your services
Nextdoor, Facebook groups, Google Business Profile. Post before/after photos everywhere. Put yard signs during jobs!
‚úì
Step 7: Deliver professional results
Clean lines, no drips, thorough prep. Protect floors and furniture. Leave it better than you found it. Ask for reviews!
‚úì
Step 8: Scale your business
Hire helpers for large jobs. Partner with realtors (move-in/out painting). Add cabinet painting for premium jobs!
